Korean Fried Chicken (Dakgangjeong)

Why This Recipe

You've been exploring Korean cuisine — this is the iconic crispy, sticky, gochujang-glazed chicken that defines Korean street food. The cornflour coating gives an impossibly crunchy texture, and the sweet-spicy sauce is addictive. A proper step up from takeaway.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken wings
  • 1 large chunk ginger, peeled and finely grated
  • 50g cornflour
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Sesame seeds and sliced spring onion, to serve
  • 6 tbsp dark brown soft sugar
  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean chilli paste)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 large garlic cloves, crushed
  • Small piece of ginger, peeled and grated
  • 2 tsp sesame oil

Instructions

Make the Sauce

  • Put the dark brown sugar, gochujang, soy sauce, crushed garlic, grated ginger, and sesame oil in a small saucepan.
  • Simmer over medium heat for 3-4 minutes until the mixture thickens to a syrupy glaze.
  • Remove from heat and set aside.

Prep the Chicken

  • Season the chicken wings generously with salt, pepper, and the grated ginger.
  • Place the cornflour in a large bowl or zip-lock bag.
  • Toss the chicken wings in the cornflour until completely and evenly coated — shake off any excess.

Fry

  • Heat about 2cm of vegetable oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat (around 170°C/340°F).
  • Carefully add the coated chicken wings in a single layer — don't overcrowd the pan.
  • Fry for 8-10 minutes until golden brown and crisp, turning halfway through.
  • Remove from the oil and drain on kitchen paper. Let cool slightly for about 2 minutes.

Glaze and Serve

  • Reheat the sauce gently in the saucepan.
  • Toss the crispy chicken wings in the warm sauce until evenly coated and sticky.
  • Transfer to a serving bowl and scatter over sesame seeds and sliced spring onions.
  • Serve immediately while still crispy.

Pro Tips

  • For extra crunch, double-fry the chicken: fry once at 160°C, rest for 5 minutes, then fry again at 180°C for 2 minutes before glazing.
  • Swap chicken wings for drumsticks or thigh pieces if you prefer meatier bites — just extend frying time by 3-4 minutes.
