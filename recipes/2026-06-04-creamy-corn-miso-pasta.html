Creamy Corn Miso Pasta

Why This Recipe

Summer corn at its peak, turned into a silky pasta sauce with white miso and a touch of gochugaru heat. The corn milk scraped from the cobs is liquid gold — it gives the sauce body and intense corn flavour without a drop of cream. Quick, vegetarian, and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz campanelle pasta (fusilli or cavatappi work)
  • 2 ears fresh corn, shucked
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 yellow onion, finely chopped (~1 cup)
  • 2 small garlic cloves, grated or finely minced
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1/2 tsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) — red pepper flakes work fine
  • 4 tbsp fresh chives, chopped (divided)
  • 1 oz Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated (~1/4 cup), plus extra for serving
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • Kosher salt, to taste

Instructions

Prep

  • Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a rolling boil.
  • Stand corn ears upright in a shallow bowl. Slice kernels downward into the bowl. Scrape cobs with the back of a knife to extract corn milk. Yield: ~1½ cups kernels + milk.

Cook

  • Cook pasta al dente (8–10 mins). Reserve 1 cup starchy water, drain, and set aside.
  • Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook onion until translucent (~3 mins). Add garlic, cook 1 min.
  • Add corn with milk, cook 3 mins until slightly softened and pan looks dry.
  • Add 1/2 cup water. Cook 3–4 mins until corn is tender and water mostly evaporated.

Sauce

  • Remove from heat. Reserve 1/2 cup corn mixture for garnish.
  • Process remaining mixture until almost smooth (~20 sec). Pulse in 1/4 cup reserved mixture for a silky, slightly textured sauce.
  • Return sauce to skillet, warm 1 min. Whisk in white miso + 1/2 cup pasta water until smooth. Stir in gochugaru.

Finish

  • Stir in drained pasta and 3 tbsp chives. Toss 1 min until fully coated.
  • Off heat, gradually stir in Parmigiano-Reggiano, adding remaining pasta water 1 tbsp at a time until creamy and glossy.
  • Stir in lemon juice, adjust salt if needed.

Serve

  • Divide into 4 bowls. Top with remaining chives, reserved corn, and extra cheese. Serve immediately.

Pro Tips

  • Scrape that cob. The corn milk (the milky liquid left on the cob after slicing off kernels) is liquid gold — it adds body and intense corn flavour to the sauce. Do not skip scraping with the back of your knife.
  • Whisk the miso in, do not dump it. White miso is thick and does not dissolve on its own. Whisk it into warm pasta water before adding it back to the pan so it distributes evenly without clumping.
  • If you cannot find gochugaru, regular red pepper flakes or a pinch of cayenne work perfectly.
  • Do not go too heavy on the miso — a little goes a long way.
