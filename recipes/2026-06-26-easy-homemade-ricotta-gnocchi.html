Easy Homemade Ricotta Gnocchi

Why This Recipe

Ricotta gnocchi is 'Italian fast food' — pillowy dumplings made from scratch in 30 minutes with no potatoes, no resting, no fuss. A new technique that's surprisingly approachable and delivers restaurant-quality results.

Ingredients

  • 250g / 8oz full-fat ricotta (not the soft spreadable kind)
  • 3/4 cup (75g) freshly grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg (55-60g)
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1/4 tsp cooking salt
  • Pinch of black pepper
  • 3/4 to 1 cup plain/all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 4 tbsp / 60g unsalted butter
  • 1/4 tsp salt (omit if using salted butter)
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 onion, finely diced
  • 28 oz / 800g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp mixed dried Italian herbs
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Freshly grated parmesan, for serving
  • Fresh parsley, finely chopped (optional)

Instructions

Make the Gnocchi

  • Put all gnocchi ingredients in a bowl except the flour. Use a rubber spatula or wooden spoon to mix until combined — smear up the sides of the bowl to help cream the ricotta.
  • Add 3/4 cup of flour and mix until just combined — you want a very sticky dough that holds its shape in a mound but is still soft. Stickier dough = softer gnocchi. Add remaining flour 1 tbsp at a time only if needed.
  • Sprinkle a work surface with flour, then scrape the dough out onto it. Shape into a disc about 2.5cm / 1 inch thick. Cut into 8 wedges.
  • Roll each wedge into a log about 25cm / 10 inches long and 1.25cm / 1/2 inch wide. Cut into 1.5cm / 3/5 inch pieces. (You can freeze uncooked gnocchi at this point.)

Cook the Gnocchi

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  • Gently tumble the gnocchi in and cook for about 2.5 minutes, or until they float to the surface for around 30 seconds.
  • Reserve 1 mug of the cooking water, then drain the gnocchi.

Sauce Option 1: Browned Butter (Quick)

  • Melt the butter in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Let it bubble until it turns golden brown with a nutty aroma.
  • Add salt and pepper. If gnocchi isn't ready yet, remove from heat temporarily.
  • Pour the cooked gnocchi into the pan along with 1/2 cup of the reserved cooking water. Gently stir, coating the gnocchi in the browned butter for 1 minute — the butter and starch emulsify to create a silky sauce.

Sauce Option 2: Tomato (Hearty)

  • Heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add garlic and onion, sauté for 3-5 minutes until the onion is translucent.
  • Add crushed tomatoes, Italian herbs, and salt. Simmer for 10 minutes, tasting and adjusting seasoning.
  • Add the gnocchi and 1/2 cup of reserved cooking water. Cook for 1 minute, stirring gently — the oil and starch will emulsify, thickening the sauce so it clings to each piece.

Serve

  • Serve immediately — gnocchi starts to firm up after about 15 minutes as it cools.
  • Garnish with freshly grated parmesan and chopped parsley if desired.

Pro Tips

  • Use regular full-fat ricotta, not the soft spreadable kind — the lumpy texture is key for the right dough consistency.
  • Stickier dough = softer gnocchi. Don't be tempted to add too much flour — the dough should be soft and slightly tacky, not stiff.
  • Save that cooking water! The starch is what makes the sauce silky and helps it cling to the gnocchi.
