Laksa Noodle Soup (Malaysian Coconut Curry Noodle Soup)

Why This Recipe

Laksa is the iconic Malaysian spicy coconut noodle soup — an incredibly rich, fragrant, complex broth loaded with classic toppings. A natural next step for someone who loved Thai green curry, introducing a whole new Southeast Asian flavour world. Made accessible with store-bought laksa paste elevated with fresh aromatics.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (500 ml) chicken stock / broth
  • 1 cup (250 ml) water
  • 3 chicken drumsticks
  • 1 1/2 tbsp oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cm piece of ginger, finely grated
  • 1 lemongrass stalk, white part only, grated
  • 2 bird's eye chillies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup (135g) laksa paste
  • 400ml can coconut milk
  • 2 tsp fish sauce (or soy sauce)
  • 50g vermicelli noodles, dried
  • 100g hokkien noodles, dried (optional)
  • 80g bean sprouts
  • 80g tofu puffs, cut in half
  • Fresh coriander / cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Crispy fried shallots, optional
  • Finely sliced red chilli, optional

Instructions

Poach Chicken & Make Stock

  • Place chicken stock, water, chicken drumsticks, and 1 1/2 tbsp oil in a medium saucepan over high heat.
  • Bring to a simmer, then reduce to medium-high heat.
  • Cook for 25 minutes or until chicken flesh is falling off the bone and liquid reduces by about one-third.
  • Discard the chicken skin, pull all flesh off the bones, and place shredded chicken in a bowl. Discard bones. Set the broth aside.

Make Laksa Chilli Sauce

  • In a small bowl, combine 1/2 tsp sugar, 1 1/2 tsp soy sauce, 1/2 garlic clove (minced), 1 1/2 tsp laksa paste, 1 tbsp Sriracha sauce, 1 tbsp chilli paste, and 1 tbsp vegetable oil.
  • Mix well and set aside for 20 minutes to let the flavours meld.

Build the Laksa Broth

  • Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium-low heat. Add 2 minced garlic cloves and grated ginger, sauté for 20 seconds.
  • Add grated lemongrass and chopped bird's eye chillies. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  • Add 1/2 cup laksa paste. Turn heat up to medium and cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until deeply fragrant.
  • Pour in the reserved chicken stock, coconut milk, fish sauce, and 2 tsp of the Laksa Chilli Sauce.
  • Cover and simmer for 10 minutes to let the flavours meld.
  • Adjust seasoning to taste — add lime juice for sourness and more fish sauce for saltiness.
  • Add tofu puffs, turn off the heat, cover, and let rest for 5 minutes.

Cook Noodles

  • Prepare vermicelli noodles (and hokkien noodles if using) per packet directions — usually soaking in boiling water for 2-3 minutes, then draining.

Assemble & Serve

  • Divide cooked noodles between 2 deep bowls.
  • Top with shredded chicken.
  • Ladle hot laksa broth (with tofu puffs) over the chicken.
  • Top with bean sprouts, fresh coriander, and crispy fried shallots if using.
  • Serve immediately with lime wedges and the Laksa Chilli Sauce on the side for extra heat.

Pro Tips

  • The secret to restaurant-quality laksa is frying the laksa paste with fresh aromatics (garlic, ginger, lemongrass) for 2 full minutes before adding liquid — this toasts the paste and unlocks deep, complex flavours that store-bought paste alone can't deliver.
  • Add the tofu puffs at the very end and let them rest off-heat for 5 minutes — they act like sponges, soaking up the rich broth without becoming mushy.
