Nasi Goreng (Indonesian Fried Rice)

Why This Recipe

Indonesian fried rice is a quick, flavour-packed weeknight meal that's all about the caramelized colour and deep umami from kecap manis (sweet soy sauce). Perfect for using up leftover rice, and the bold spices are a welcome change from the Korean-heavy recent recommendations.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil, divided
  • 150g chicken breast, thinly sliced (or use tofu/prawns)
  • 3 tbsp kecap manis (Indonesian sweet soy sauce), divided
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp red chilli, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 cups cooked white rice, cold and day-old
  • 2 tsp shrimp paste (terasi), optional but recommended
  • 4 eggs, fried sunny-side up
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato, cut into wedges
  • Fried shallots for garnish (optional)
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions

Prep

  • Slice chicken breast thinly against the grain. Pat rice dry with a paper towel if moist.
  • Finely chop garlic, red chilli, and onion. Slice green onions.
  • Cut cucumber and tomato into wedges for serving. Have lime wedges ready.

Cook the Chicken

  • Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  • Add chilli and garlic, stir for 10 seconds until fragrant — do not let garlic brown.
  • Add diced onion, cook for 1 minute until just softened.
  • Add sliced chicken, stir-fry until mostly white (about 2 minutes).
  • Add 1 tbsp kecap manis, toss to coat chicken. Cook 1 more minute until chicken is caramelized and cooked through.
  • Remove chicken from wok and set aside on a plate.

Fry the Rice

  • Add remaining 1.5 tbsp oil to the wok over high heat.
  • Add cold rice, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir-fry for 1 minute.
  • Add remaining 2 tbsp kecap manis and shrimp paste (if using). Stir constantly for 2 minutes.
  • The rice should turn a deep caramelized brown — this is the key step for authentic flavour. Keep stirring so it doesn't burn.
  • Return the chicken to the wok, toss everything together for 30 seconds.

Fry the Eggs

  • While rice is cooking, heat a separate small pan with a little oil over medium heat.
  • Crack eggs into the pan, fry sunny-side up until whites are set but yolks are still runny (about 2-3 minutes).

Serve

  • Divide rice among 4 plates.
  • Top each portion with a fried egg — the runny yolk mixes into the rice for richness.
  • Garnish with sliced green onions, fried shallots if using.
  • Serve with cucumber and tomato wedges on the side, plus lime wedges for squeezing over the top.

Pro Tips

  • Cold, day-old rice is non-negotiable — freshly cooked rice is too moist and will turn mushy. If you don't have leftover rice, cook it ahead and spread it on a tray to cool quickly, then refrigerate for at least an hour.
  • If you can't find kecap manis, make your own: simmer 3 tbsp regular soy sauce with 2 tbsp brown sugar until thickened to a syrupy consistency. It won't be identical but gets close.
  • Shrimp paste (terasi) is sold in small blocks in Asian supermarkets. It's intense — start with 1 tsp and taste before adding more. Toast it briefly in a dry pan before using for deeper flavour.
