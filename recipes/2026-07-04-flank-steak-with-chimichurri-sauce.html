Flank Steak with Chimichurri Sauce

Why This Recipe

Summer grilling season is here and chimichurri — that vibrant, herb-packed Argentinian condiment — is the perfect way to bring bold, fresh flavors to a simple steak. A new technique and flavor profile that's quick enough for a weeknight.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds flank steak
  • 1/4 cup olive oil (for marinade)
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, packed
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, packed
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon Italian seasoning blend
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1/3 cup olive oil (for chimichurri)
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (for chimichurri, or to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper (for chimichurri, or to taste)

Instructions

Marinate

  • Whisk together 1/4 cup olive oil, chili powder, onion powder, 1 teaspoon salt, and lime juice in a small bowl to make the marinade.
  • Place the flank steak in a large ziplock bag, pour the marinade over the steak, seal the bag, and massage to coat evenly.
  • Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight for deeper flavor penetration.

Make the Chimichurri

  • Combine cilantro, parsley, diced onion, garlic, Italian seasoning, crushed red pepper flakes, 1/3 cup olive oil, red wine vinegar, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper in a blender or food processor.
  • Pulse until smooth but still slightly textured — about 10-15 seconds. Taste and adjust salt and pepper if needed.
  • Set aside at room temperature while you cook the steak — the flavors meld as it sits.

Cook the Steak

  • Remove steak from the marinade and let it sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before cooking (helps it cook more evenly).
  • Preheat a heavy skillet (cast iron is ideal) or outdoor grill to medium-high heat. Add a thin layer of oil to the pan.
  • Sear the steak for 6-8 minutes on each side until heavily browned on the outside. Do not move it during searing — let the crust form.
  • Check doneness: the center should be slightly pink (medium-rare to medium, about 130-135°F internal temperature). Use a meat thermometer for precision.

Rest and Serve

  • Transfer the steak to a cutting board and let it rest for 5-10 minutes — this is critical, it lets the juices redistribute so the meat stays tender.
  • Thinly slice the steak across the grain (perpendicular to the muscle fibers) — this is the key to tender flank steak.
  • Arrange sliced steak on a platter and generously spoon chimichurri sauce over the top.
  • Serve immediately with crusty bread to soak up the sauce, or alongside roasted potatoes or a simple green salad.

Pro Tips

  • Always slice flank steak across the grain — cutting perpendicular to the long muscle fibers is what transforms a potentially tough cut into something melt-in-your-mouth tender.
  • Make extra chimichurri — it keeps in the fridge for up to 2 weeks and gets better with age. Use it on grilled vegetables, mixed into rice, or as a dip with crusty bread.
