Kuku Sabzi (Persian Herb Frittata)

Why This Recipe

A vibrant Persian herb frittata packed with fresh dill, parsley, and cilantro — perfect for peak summer herbs. It's a hands-off bake that's equally delicious hot or cold, making it ideal for a relaxed summer dinner.

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup fresh parsley leaves (including tender stems)
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro/coriander (including tender stems)
  • 1 cup fresh dill
  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • 1 cup scallions/green onions, sliced (about 4)
  • ⅓ cup walnuts, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon dried fenugreek (optional)
  • ¼ cup barberries/zereshk (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

Prep

  • Wash all fresh herbs and spinach thoroughly, then dry completely using a salad spinner or kitchen towels — removing all moisture is critical for the right texture.
  • Chop the parsley, cilantro, and dill finely with a sharp knife (avoid a food processor, which can turn the herbs mushy).
  • Slice the scallions/green onions into thin rounds.
  • Finely chop the walnuts. For extra flavor, toast them in a dry saucepan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.

Mix

  • In a large bowl, whisk the 4 eggs until just combined — don't over-beat.
  • Add turmeric powder, baking powder, flour, ½ teaspoon salt, and a generous pinch of black pepper. Whisk to combine.
  • Fold in all the chopped herbs, spinach, scallions, fenugreek (if using), chopped walnuts, and barberries (if using). Stir gently until just combined — the mixture should be a thick green batter. If too wet, add another tablespoon of flour.

Cook

  • Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a non-stick 9.5-inch (24 cm) pan over low-medium heat.
  • Pour the herb batter into the pan and quickly spread it evenly with a rubber spatula.
  • Cook for about 15 minutes. The top and edges should set and pull away from the sides. Move the edges with the spatula occasionally to prevent sticking.
  • Place a large plate over the pan and carefully flip the kuku onto the plate — the cooked side should be dark green, almost black.
  • Drizzle a little more oil into the pan, then slide the kuku back in (uncooked side down) to cook the second side for about 10 minutes.
  • Check a corner to ensure it's golden brown, then flip back onto the plate.

Serve

  • Let the kuku rest for at least 10 minutes before slicing — this helps it set and prevents crumbling.
  • Slice into squares or triangles and garnish with extra chopped walnuts and barberries.
  • Serve warm or at room temperature alongside fresh bread, yogurt, or a simple salad.

Pro Tips

  • You can prep the herbs the night before — wash, chop, and dry them, then store in the fridge. The extra drying time actually improves the final texture.
  • Don't skip the resting time after cooking — slicing too soon will cause the kuku to fall apart. It's also delicious cold, making it great for meal prep.
