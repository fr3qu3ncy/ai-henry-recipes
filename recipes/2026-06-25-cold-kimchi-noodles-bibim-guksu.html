Cold Kimchi Noodles (Bibim Guksu)

Why This Recipe

Korean bibim guksu is the ultimate summer dish — cold, spicy, tangy noodles that come together in 30 minutes. Perfect for hot weather when you want something refreshing but deeply flavourful, and a great next step in your Korean cooking journey.

Ingredients

  • 150g kimchi, chopped (drained weight)
  • 4 tbsp kimchi brine
  • 2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds, toasted
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chilli paste)
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 200g dried thin wheat noodles (somyeon or somen noodles)
  • 1/2 cucumber, cut into thin matchsticks
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, halved

Instructions

Prep

  • Hard-boil the eggs: place in a pan of cold water, bring to a boil, then simmer for 8-9 minutes. Transfer to ice water, peel, and halve.
  • Cut the cucumber into thin matchsticks using a peeler or mandoline. Set aside in ice water for extra crunch.
  • Chop the kimchi into bite-sized pieces. Reserve the brine from the kimchi jar — you'll need 4 tablespoons of it.

Make the Sauce

  • In a medium mixing bowl, combine the chopped kimchi, kimchi brine, toasted sesame oil, toasted sesame seeds, gochujang, sugar, and crushed garlic.
  • Stir well until the sugar dissolves and the sauce is smooth and glossy. Taste and adjust — add more gochujang for extra heat or more sugar for balance.
  • Set the sauce aside while you cook the noodles.

Cook the Noodles

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the noodles according to packet instructions, usually 3-5 minutes for thin wheat noodles.
  • Drain the noodles and immediately rinse under cold running water, stirring with your hands until completely cool to the touch. This stops the cooking and gives them that signature chewy texture.
  • Drain thoroughly — excess water will dilute the sauce.

Assemble & Serve

  • Add the cold noodles directly to the kimchi sauce bowl. Toss vigorously with your hands or tongs until every strand is coated in the spicy sauce.
  • Divide the noodles between two serving bowls.
  • Top with cucumber matchsticks and halved hard-boiled eggs.
  • Serve immediately while the noodles are still cold and the sauce is at its peak flavour.

Pro Tips

  • Toast the sesame seeds in a dry pan over medium heat for 1-2 minutes until fragrant — it makes a huge difference to the nutty aroma.
  • For extra protein, add a handful of shredded chicken or some crispy Korean fried tofu (yubu). The dish is versatile enough to handle almost any topping.
