Ahi Tuna Aguachile

Why This Recipe

Aguachile is Mexico's answer to ceviche — a no-cook dish of raw fish 'cooked' in citrus and chile. This ahi tuna version is fresh, herby, and spicy, perfect for hot July days when the last thing you want to do is turn on the oven. Zero cook time, all flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup packed fresh cilantro leaves and stems, plus more for serving
  • 1/2 cup fresh lime juice (from about 4 limes)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 serrano peppers (1 for sauce, 1 for garnish), halved and seeded
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 6 oz sushi-grade ahi tuna, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1 small avocado, thinly sliced
  • Tortilla chips, for serving (optional)

Instructions

Prep the Onion

  • Fill a small bowl with cold water. Add the thinly sliced red onion and set aside to soak — this mellows the sharp bite while keeping it crisp.

Make the Aguachile Sauce

  • In a food processor or blender, combine the cilantro, lime juice, soy sauce, garlic, 1 serrano pepper (halved and seeded), 2 tablespoons water, and the salt.
  • Blend until mostly smooth but some texture remains, about 30 seconds.
  • Taste and adjust — add more salt for brightness or more lime for acidity if needed.

Slice the Tuna

  • Using a very clean, very sharp knife, slice the ahi tuna thinly against the grain.
  • Keep the sliced tuna in the fridge until you're ready to assemble — raw fish should stay cold until the moment of serving.
  • The thin slices will 'cook' slightly in the lime juice, turning from translucent red to a pale pink at the edges.

Assemble

  • Spread a generous layer of the green aguachile sauce on the bottom of a large rimmed plate or shallow bowl.
  • Arrange the tuna slices in a single layer on top of the sauce.
  • Top with the thinly sliced avocado.
  • Drain the soaked red onion and pat dry with paper towels. Layer the onion on top of the avocado.
  • Thinly slice the remaining serrano pepper and scatter over the top.
  • Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

Serve

  • Serve immediately while the tuna is still cold and fresh.
  • Place alongside tortilla chips for scooping up the sauce and fish.
  • The lime juice will continue to 'cook' the fish, so don't let it sit — eat it right away for the best texture.

Pro Tips

  • Sushi-grade fish is essential — 'sushi-grade' and 'sashimi-grade' aren't FDA-regulated terms, so buy from a trusted fishmonger and ask specifically for fish safe to eat raw. It's a splurge but worth it.
  • The aguachile sauce keeps for 4-5 days in an airtight container in the fridge — make a double batch and use it on shrimp, scallops, or white fish for variety.
  • Soaking the red onion in cold water is non-negotiable — it removes the harsh bite while keeping the crunch and color. Pat dry before adding to prevent watery sauce.
