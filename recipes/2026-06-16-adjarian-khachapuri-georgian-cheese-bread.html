Adjarian Khachapuri (Georgian Cheese Bread)

Why This Recipe

Georgia's most iconic dish — a boat-shaped bread stuffed with molten cheese, topped with a runny egg and a knob of butter. You stir it all together and dip the crust in. It's a complete new cuisine (Georgian), a showstopper technique, and surprisingly accessible with store-bought pizza dough. Perfect for brunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 tbsp onion salt (or 1 tbsp dried dill + 1 tsp salt)
  • 2 eggs, plus 1 egg beaten for egg wash
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, cubed
  • 2 tsp Everything But The Bagel Seasoning (optional, for crust)
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 2 tbsp green onion, finely chopped
  • Touch of olive oil, for coating dough

Instructions

Rest the Dough

  • Take cold pizza dough out of the fridge and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Swirl to coat.
  • Cover with a clean tea towel and let rest at room temperature for 1 hour. This relaxes the gluten so the dough becomes pliable and easy to shape without springing back.
  • After resting, punch down the dough to release any air bubbles.

Prep the Cheese Filling

  • In a medium bowl, combine the shredded mozzarella, crumbled feta, ricotta, and onion salt (or dried dill + salt). Mix gently until evenly distributed.
  • The three-cheese blend is key: mozzarella gives stretch, feta adds saltiness, and ricotta brings softness and body.

Preheat

  • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). If using a convection/fan oven, this is fine. If using a conventional/still oven, increase to 375°F (190°C).
  • Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

Shape the Khachapuri

  • Divide the dough into 2 equal portions (for 2 individual khachapuri) or keep as 1 large one to share.
  • Roll each dough ball into a thin oval, about 9-10 inches long and 1/8 inch thick. A lightly floured surface helps.
  • Place the rolled oval onto the parchment-lined baking sheet.
  • Spoon the cheese filling down the centre of the dough, leaving a 1/2-inch border around all edges. Do not press the cheese into the dough — just lay it on top so the bread underneath stays fluffy.
  • Roll the left long edge of the dough inward toward the cheese, then do the same on the right side. The dough should overlap slightly at the centre, forming a boat shape with the cheese exposed in the middle.
  • Pinch and twist both narrow ends of the dough twice to seal them shut — this keeps the cheese from leaking out during baking.
  • Brush the exposed dough edges with beaten egg wash for a golden finish.
  • Sprinkle Everything But The Bagel Seasoning (or coarse garlic powder) over the egg-washed edges for flavour and crunch.

Bake

  • Bake for 20 minutes until the dough edges are golden brown and puffed, and the cheese is bubbling.
  • Remove from the oven. Crack an egg directly into the centre of each khachapuri, right on top of the molten cheese. Drop a cube of butter into the centre too.
  • Return to the oven and bake for 5 more minutes until the egg white is just set but the yolk is still runny. Watch closely — the last 2 minutes go fast.

Serve

  • Remove from the oven and let rest for 1-2 minutes — just long enough to handle but still piping hot.
  • Sprinkle with red pepper flakes (for heat) and finely chopped green onion (for freshness).
  • Stir the egg yolk, butter, and cheese together right at the table — this creates a rich, creamy, lava-like filling.
  • Tear off pieces of the golden crust and dip them into the cheese. Eat immediately while it's still gooey.

Pro Tips

  • The three-cheese blend is non-negotiable for authentic flavour. Georgian khachapuri traditionally uses sulguni (a Georgian semi-hard cheese), but the mozzarella + feta + ricotta combo is the closest accessible substitute and delivers the same stretch-salt-cream balance.
  • Don't skip resting the dough — cold pizza dough is tight and will spring back when you try to roll it. An hour at room temperature makes it completely pliable.
  • Serve with a simple side of blistered cherry tomatoes or a lemon-dressed arugula salad to cut through the richness. A glass of Georgian red wine (if you can find it) is the perfect pairing.
