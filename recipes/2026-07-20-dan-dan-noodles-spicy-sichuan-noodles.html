Dan Dan Noodles (Spicy Sichuan Noodles)

Why This Recipe

A fiery Sichuan classic with numbing Sichuan peppercorns, rich sesame-chilli sauce, and silky noodles — an intensely flavour-packed bowl that's become one of the most popular Chinese dishes worldwide. Perfect for Martin's love of bold, aromatic spice.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp Chinese sesame paste
  • 1.5 tbsp Chinese chilli paste in oil (, adjust spiciness )
  • 4 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves (, minced)
  • 3 tsp white sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Chinese five spice powder
  • 1 tsp Sichuan pepper powder (, preferably freshly ground )
  • 3 tbsp (or more!) chilli oil (, preferably Chinese )
  • 3/4 cup (185ml) chicken broth/stock (, hot, low sodium)
  • 2 tsp Hoisin sauce
  • 1 tsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Chinese cooking wine
  • 1/2 tsp Chinese five spice powder
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 250g/ 8oz pork mince
  • 1 tsp vegetable oil
  • 30g (1/4 cup) Sui Mi Ya Cai (preserved mustard greens) (, finely chopped )
  • 500g/1lb white fresh noodles (, medium thickness )
  • 16 choy sum stems (, cut into 15cm pieces)
  • 2 green onions (, finely sliced)
  • 1 tbsp peanuts (, finely chopped )

Instructions

Make the Dan Dan Sauce

  • In a bowl, combine 2 tbsp Chinese sesame paste, 1.5 tbsp Chinese chilli paste in oil, 4 tbsp light soy sauce, 2 minced garlic cloves, 3 tsp white sugar, 1/2 tsp Chinese five spice powder, and 1 tsp Sichuan pepper powder.
  • Gently stir in 3 tbsp chilli oil and 3/4 cup hot chicken stock — the oil should be sitting on the surface. Set aside.

Cook the Pork

  • In a small bowl, mix together 2 tsp Hoisin sauce, 1 tsp dark soy sauce, 1 tbsp Chinese cooking wine, and 1/2 tsp Chinese five spice powder to make the pork sauce.
  • Heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil in a skillet or wok over high heat.
  • Add 250g pork mince and cook, breaking it up with a spatula, until it changes from pink to white and is nicely browned.
  • Pour in the pork sauce and cook for 1 minute, stirring to coat. Transfer the pork to a bowl and set aside.

Warm the Sui Mi Ya Cai

  • Return the skillet to medium heat. Add 1 tsp vegetable oil to the centre.
  • Add 30g Sui Mi Ya Cai (preserved mustard greens) and stir-fry for 30 seconds just to warm through. Set aside.

Cook the Noodles

  • Bring a large pot of water to the boil. Cook 500g white fresh noodles according to packet directions (usually 2-3 minutes).
  • Add 16 choy sum stems (cut into 15cm pieces) during the last 1 minute of cooking.
  • Drain well.

Assemble & Serve

  • Ladle about 1/4 of the Dan Dan Sauce into each serving bowl.
  • Pile in the cooked noodles, top with the seasoned pork and Sui Mi Ya Cai.
  • Arrange the choy sum on the side.
  • Sprinkle with finely sliced green onions and chopped peanuts.
  • Mix everything together at the table to coat the noodles well with sauce, then enjoy!

Pro Tips

  • Toast Sichuan peppercorns in a dry pan for 30 seconds before grinding — it unlocks the signature numbing sensation (mala) that defines Sichuan cuisine.
  • If you can't find Sui Mi Ya Cai, substitute with finely chopped pickled vegetables or sauerkraut — it adds the essential preserved, tangy crunch.
  • The Dan Dan Sauce keeps for a week in the fridge — make a double batch and use it for quick noodle bowls any night of the week.
