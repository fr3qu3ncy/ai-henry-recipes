Grilled Peach and Burrata Salad

Why This Recipe

Grilled peaches bring caramelised sweetness to a salad that balances rich burrata, salty prosciutto and bright herbs.

Ingredients

  • For Grilling:
  • 2½ lbs peaches (about 7), pitted and quartered
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • For the Salad:
  • ½ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar or white wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp finely minced fresh chives
  • 8 oz burrata, brought to room temperature
  • ½ cup torn fresh basil
  • 2 oz thinly sliced prosciutto, torn into pieces
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions

Prep

  • Select peaches that are ripe but still firm — they should yield slightly to gentle pressure but not feel mushy. Overripe peaches will fall apart on the grill; underripe ones won't soften or sweeten properly.
  • Pit and quarter the peaches (cut into quarters, not halves — this exposes more surface for char and makes flipping easier).
  • Bring the burrata to room temperature at least 30 minutes before serving. Cold burrata won't spread and mutes its richness.
  • Whisk together the olive oil, rice vinegar, fish sauce, and minced chives in a small bowl. Set aside.

Grill

  • Set up a two-zone fire on your grill.
  • Charcoal: Fill a chimney starter ¾ full. Once ashed over, pour coals evenly over half the grate. Leave the other half bare.
  • Gas: Preheat all burners on high (~500°F) for 15 minutes. Keep one burner on high, turn off the other(s) to create an indirect zone.
  • Clean and oil the grate thoroughly.
  • Brush the cut sides of the peach quarters with melted butter.
  • Place peaches cut-side down on the hot zone. Cook 5–7 minutes until deep grill marks form.
  • Flip the peaches and move them to the cooler zone. Cook another 3–7 minutes until tender, lightly marked on the second side, and slightly translucent at the edges. Total grill time is about 10 minutes.
  • Transfer peaches to a plate and let them cool for a few minutes.

Assemble

  • Place the burrata on a large platter. Cut or tear it into 8–10 pieces so the creamy stracciatella center can ooze across the plate.
  • Arrange the warm peach quarters around and over the burrata.
  • Scatter the torn prosciutto and fresh basil across the top.
  • Drizzle the vinaigrette evenly over everything.
  • Finish with a generous crack of black pepper.
  • Serve immediately — the contrast of warm peaches, room-temp burrata, and crisp herbs is best right at assembly.
