Korean Fried Chicken (Dakgangjeong)

Why This Recipe

Korean fried chicken is legendary for its ultra-crispy, double-fried coating that stays crunchy even the next day. Perfect for your Korean cuisine exploration - this introduces the double-fry technique and the iconic sweet-spicy-savory glaze that makes dakgangjeong a street food icon.

Ingredients

  • 3.5 lbs chicken wings (about 1.6 kg), washed and drained
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp minced ginger
  • 2/3 cup potato starch or corn starch
  • 1/3 cup peanuts (optional)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3-4 large dried red chili peppers, seeded, cut into 1/3 inch pieces (optional)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup rice syrup or corn syrup
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tbsp mustard (optional)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • Grapeseed oil (or vegetable/peanut oil) for deep frying

Instructions

Prep

  • Cut off the tip of each chicken wing and chop each wing in half (drumette and flat). You should have about 3 lbs of chicken, 24-26 pieces.
  • Put chicken in a large bowl and mix with salt, ginger, and ground black pepper by hand.
  • Put potato starch in a shallow bowl. Dip each wing piece in the starch one by one, squeezing to press the coating on tightly.

Make the Sauce

  • Heat a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add 2 tbsp cooking oil, minced garlic, and dried red chili peppers.
  • Stir with a wooden spoon until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  • Add soy sauce, rice syrup, vinegar, and mustard (if using). Stir and let it bubble for a few minutes.
  • Add brown sugar and continue stirring. Remove from heat and set aside.

First Fry

  • Pour 4 cups of cooking oil into a deep frying pan or pot. Heat over high heat for 7-8 minutes.
  • Test oil temperature by dipping one wing in - if the oil bubbles vigorously, it is ready.
  • Slide coated wings into hot oil one by one. Deep fry for 12-13 minutes, turning a few times with tongs.
  • Remove wings, drain on a wire rack or strainer. Turn off heat and let wings rest for a few minutes.

Second Fry

  • Reheat the oil to high heat again.
  • Fry the wings a second time for 12-15 minutes until golden brown and super crunchy through the tongs.
  • If your pan is small, fry in batches. If using peanuts, carefully dip them in hot oil for 15-30 seconds until light golden brown, then transfer to a small bowl.

Coat and Serve

  • Reheat the sauce until it bubbles.
  • Add the hot fried chicken and peanuts (if using) to the sauce. Mix well with a wooden spoon to coat evenly.
  • Transfer to a large platter. Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and serve immediately.

Pro Tips

  • The double-fry technique is the secret: the first fry cooks the chicken through, the second fry drives off remaining moisture for an impossibly crispy shell that stays crunchy even the next day.
  • Potato starch creates a lighter, crispier coating than regular flour. If you cannot find it, corn starch works well as a substitute.
