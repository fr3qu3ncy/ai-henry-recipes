Korean-Style Shakshuka with Gochujang & Kimchi

Why This Recipe

A brilliant fusion of Middle Eastern shakshuka with Korean flavors — gochujang, red miso, and kimchi create a deeply savory, spicy sauce. Ready in 20 minutes, perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or a lazy brunch.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1-2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste), to taste
  • 3 tablespoons red miso paste, divided
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 4 large eggs
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • 2-3 scallions, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup kimchi, roughly chopped, for garnish
  • Crusty bread, toast, or biscuits for serving

Instructions

Prep

  • Thinly slice the scallions, separating whites and greens if desired.
  • Roughly chop the kimchi and set aside.
  • Have all ingredients measured and within reach — this comes together quickly.

Cook the Sauce

  • Heat sesame oil in a large skillet (cast iron works well) over medium-high heat.
  • Once the oil is shimmering, add gochujang, 2 tablespoons red miso paste, and soy sauce. Stir constantly for about 1 minute until fragrant and the paste bubbles slightly.
  • Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir to combine. Reduce heat to medium-low.
  • Simmer for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly and the flavors meld.
  • Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning — add more gochujang for heat, more soy sauce for saltiness, or the remaining 1 tablespoon miso for extra umami.

Poach the Eggs

  • Use a spoon to make 4 small wells in the sauce, spaced evenly across the skillet.
  • Crack one egg into each well, being careful not to break the yolks.
  • Cover the skillet with a lid and cook for 5-10 minutes — whites should be fully set, yolks still runny (5-7 min) or jammy (8-10 min), to your preference.
  • Remove from heat. Taste the sauce one final time and adjust salt/seasoning if needed.

Serve

  • Sprinkle generously with sesame seeds and sliced scallions.
  • Top each egg with a dollop of kimchi.
  • Serve straight from the skillet with crusty bread, toast, or biscuits for dipping into the sauce.

Pro Tips

  • Batch-make the gochujang-miso tomato sauce and freeze in portions — you can have shakshuka on demand by just cracking eggs into the reheated sauce.
  • If you want to stretch it into a full meal, add a handful of spinach or frozen peas during the last minute of simmering, or fold in sliced cooked chicken or tofu.
