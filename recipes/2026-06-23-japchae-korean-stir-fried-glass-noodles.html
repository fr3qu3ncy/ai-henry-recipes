Japchae (Korean Stir-Fried Glass Noodles)

Why This Recipe

A Korean classic that's perfect for summer — chewy sweet potato noodles tossed with tender beef, crisp vegetables, and a savoury-sweet sesame-soy sauce. It's a proper technique dish that feels special but comes together in 40 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 150g dangmyun (sweet potato glass noodles)
  • 100g frying steak, cut into 5mm strips
  • 1 egg
  • 7 tbsp vegetable oil for frying
  • ½ carrot, sliced into matchsticks
  • 1 red pepper, thinly sliced
  • 100g chestnut mushrooms, thinly sliced
  • 200g spinach
  • 3 spring onions, cut into 5cm lengths
  • Toasted sesame seeds, for garnish
  • For the beef marinade: 1 tbsp sesame oil, 1 small garlic clove (finely grated), ½ tsp white pepper, 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • For the japchae sauce: 4 tbsp dark soy sauce, 2 tsp caster sugar, 2 small garlic cloves (finely grated), 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • For the spinach: ¼ tsp salt, ½ tsp sesame oil, 1 small garlic clove (finely grated), 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds

Instructions

Prep

  • Cook the sweet potato noodles following pack instructions, then drain and rinse under cold water. Set aside.
  • Mix the beef marinade ingredients together (1 tbsp sesame oil, 1 grated garlic clove, ½ tsp white pepper, 1 tbsp soy sauce, ½ tsp black pepper). Tip the beef strips into the marinade, mix thoroughly, and set aside for 10 minutes.
  • Mix the japchae sauce ingredients together (4 tbsp dark soy sauce, 2 tsp caster sugar, 2 grated garlic cloves, 1 tbsp sesame oil) and set aside.
  • Place the spinach in a large heatproof bowl and cover with boiling water. Wilt for 1 minute, then drain and rinse under cold water. Squeeze out excess water. Toss with ¼ tsp salt, ½ tsp sesame oil, 1 grated garlic clove, and 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds. Set aside.

Egg

  • Crack the egg into a small bowl and stir with a fork.
  • Place a small frying pan over medium heat and add a little vegetable oil. Pour in the egg with a pinch of salt and tilt the pan to coat it with a thin layer.
  • Fry for 30 seconds, then flip and cook the second side for 30 seconds to create a very thin omelette.
  • Remove from the pan to cool, then roll into a tube shape. Slice into thin strips, around 0.5cm thick. Set aside.

Cook

  • In a large frying pan, heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil over medium heat. Using tongs, place the marinated beef in the pan and fry for 1-2 minutes until browned. Remove and set aside.
  • Wipe the pan clean with kitchen paper and add 1 tsp oil. Tip in the carrots with a pinch of salt and fry for 2 minutes until slightly softened. Remove and set aside.
  • Wipe the pan again, add 1 tbsp oil, and fry the red peppers for 2-3 minutes until soft. Remove and set aside.
  • Wipe the pan once more, add 1 tbsp oil, and fry the mushrooms for 6-8 minutes until soft and golden brown. Remove and set aside.

Finish

  • Wipe out the pan and keep on medium heat. Pour in 2 tbsp vegetable oil.
  • When hot, tip in the cooked noodles and toss with tongs for 2 minutes to heat through.
  • Pour in the japchae sauce and add all the prepared ingredients — beef, vegetables, egg strips, spinach, and spring onions.
  • Toss everything together over high heat for a further 2 minutes, making sure all ingredients are well combined and coated with the sauce.

Serve

  • Serve immediately, sprinkled generously with toasted sesame seeds. Best enjoyed as a shared dish with rice on the side.

Pro Tips

  • Toast the sesame seeds yourself — dry-fry them in a small pan for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and lightly golden. The flavour difference over pre-toasted seeds is remarkable.
  • The key to great japchae is cooking each vegetable separately so they retain their individual textures. Don't be tempted to batch-cook them — the extra 10 minutes is worth it.
