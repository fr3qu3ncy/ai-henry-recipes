Harissa Chicken Traybake

Why This Recipe

A one-pan Mediterranean traybake that's almost entirely hands-off — 10 minutes of prep, then the oven does the work. Harissa paste (a North African chilli paste) is a game-changing ingredient you can keep in the fridge for months, and early summer's cherry tomatoes and new potatoes are at their peak right now.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken legs (drumstick + thigh portion)
  • 4 tbsp harissa paste (rose harissa is nice)
  • 1 whole garlic bulb, broken into separate cloves (skins on)
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • 400g cherry tomatoes on the vine
  • 350g new potatoes, halved if large
  • 50g Kalamata olives, roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Green salad to serve (optional)

Instructions

Prep

  • Heat oven to 190°C / 170°C fan / gas mark 5.
  • Score deep diagonal lines all over the chicken legs with a sharp knife — this helps the harissa penetrate the meat.
  • Rub the harissa paste all over the chicken legs, getting it into the scores. Season generously with salt and pepper.

Assemble

  • Place the harissa-coated chicken legs in a large roasting tin.
  • Scatter the garlic cloves (skins on) over and around the chicken — they'll roast into soft, sweet, spreadable cloves.
  • Squeeze the lemon wedges over the chicken, then tuck the wedges into the tin alongside the chicken.
  • Add the cherry tomatoes (still on the vine), halved new potatoes, and chopped olives to the tin.
  • Season everything with salt and pepper, drizzle over the olive oil, and give the tin a quick toss to mix everything together.

Cook

  • Roast in the oven for 45 minutes to 1 hour. The chicken is done when the juices run clear and the skin is golden and crispy.
  • The tomatoes will burst and caramelize, the potatoes will be tender and roasted, and the garlic cloves will be soft enough to squeeze from their skins.

Serve

  • Transfer the chicken, potatoes, and tomatoes to plates. Spoon over any pan juices.
  • Squeeze the roasted garlic from its skins and dot over the dish — it's like a natural, sweet garlic butter.
  • Serve with a simple green salad if you like. Crusty bread is perfect for mopping up the harissa-tomato juices.

Pro Tips

  • Don't peel the garlic cloves — roasting them in their skins makes them impossibly soft and sweet, and they slip right out when done. Use the roasted garlic on toast, in sandwiches, or mixed into mayo.
  • Keep a tube of harissa in your fridge — it lasts for months. Use it as a marinade base, stir it into tomato sauce, or mix it with yogurt for a quick dip. Rose harissa (with preserved rose) adds a lovely floral note.
