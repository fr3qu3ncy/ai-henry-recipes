Mul Naengmyeon (Korean Cold Noodle Soup)

Why This Recipe

Korea's iconic summer noodle soup — icy-cold beef and radish kimchi broth with chewy buckwheat noodles, topped with sliced beef, pear, cucumber, and pickled radish. A refreshing, cooling dish that's perfect for June and introduces a completely new technique: making a lightly frozen, slushy broth.

Ingredients

  • 200g dried naengmyeon noodles (Korean buckwheat noodles)
  • 150g beef brisket
  • 4 cups water (for broth)
  • 20g onion, peeled
  • 5g green onion, white part only
  • 10 whole black peppercorns
  • 2 cups dongchimi brine (radish water kimchi brine)
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar or apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Korean pear or nashi pear juice (from grated pear, strained)
  • 1 1/2 tbsp fine sugar
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1/4 tsp yellow mustard powder
  • 50g Korean pear or nashi pear, peeled, cored & thinly sliced (for topping)
  • 40g pickled radish or dongchimi radish, thinly sliced (for topping)
  • 40g cucumber, julienned (for topping)
  • 1 hard-boiled egg, halved (for topping)
  • Toasted sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Yellow mustard paste and white vinegar (to serve, optional)

Instructions

Make the Beef Broth

  • Soak the beef brisket in a bowl of cold water for 10 minutes to draw out blood, then drain.
  • Add 4 cups water, brisket, peeled onion, green onion white part, and black peppercorns to a medium pot.
  • Cover and bring to a boil over high heat. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface.
  • Once rapidly boiling, reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer covered until the brisket is tender, about 1 hour.
  • Strain the broth through a fine sieve and let it cool for 30 minutes (should yield about 3 cups).
  • Remove the meat, trim any stringy fat, and thinly slice it. Set aside.

Season and Chill the Broth

  • Combine the cooled beef stock and dongchimi brine in a bowl or container.
  • Add vinegar, pear juice, sugar, salt, and mustard powder. Stir to combine.
  • Taste and adjust seasoning — dongchimi brine strength varies, so tweak sugar and vinegar to balance sweet, sour, and savory.
  • Cover and place in the freezer for 3-4 hours until lightly frozen and slushy. Alternatively, freeze overnight and defrost until icy cold.
  • If the broth freezes solid, shave off ice with a fork to get that signature slushy texture.

Cook the Noodles

  • Boil the naengmyeon noodles following package instructions until bouncy and well-cooked.
  • Drain and rinse thoroughly under cold running water several times to cool them completely.
  • For extra chill, add ice cubes to the rinsing water.
  • Drain well and portion into serving bowls.

Assemble and Serve

  • Pour the icy-cold naengmyeon broth over the noodles in each bowl.
  • Top with sliced beef, pickled radish, cucumber, pear, and halved boiled egg.
  • Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds.
  • Serve immediately with yellow mustard paste and vinegar on the side for those who want extra zing.

Pro Tips

  • Make the broth ahead and portion it into freezer bags — it lasts months in the freezer, so you can have cold noodle soup on demand when summer cravings hit.
  • If you can't find dongchimi brine, substitute with a mix of cold beef stock and a splash of rice vinegar — it won't be authentic but still delicious. Korean pear can be replaced with Asian pear or even a Granny Smith green apple for that sweet-tart note.
