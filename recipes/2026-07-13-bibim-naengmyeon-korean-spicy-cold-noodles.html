Bibim Naengmyeon (Korean Spicy Cold Noodles)

Why This Recipe

The ultimate Korean summer dish — spicy, tangy, and refreshingly cold. Bibim Naengmyeon is something you've never cooked before: chewy buckwheat noodles tossed in a fiery gochujang-gochugaru sauce with brisket, Asian pear, and cucumber. Perfect for beating the July heat with a bold new technique.

Ingredients

  • For the beef broth & brisket:
  • 1 lb beef brisket
  • 1/4 lb Korean radish (mu), sliced
  • 1/2 small onion
  • 4 green onion bulbs (white parts only)
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp dashida (Korean beef bouillon powder)
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns
  • 4 cups water
  • For the bibim sauce:
  • 1/4 cup beef broth (from above)
  • 1/4 Asian pear, cored and chopped
  • 1/4 small onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 3-4 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 2 tbsp rice syrup
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp sea salt
  • For the noodles & toppings:
  • 1 lb chik-naengmyeon noodles (or regular naengmyeon noodles)
  • Thinly sliced cucumber
  • Asian pear, thinly sliced or cut into matchsticks
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • Crushed sesame seeds
  • Ice water for rinsing noodles

Instructions

Prep — Beef Broth & Brisket

  • Soak the brisket in cold water for 30 minutes to draw out impurities, then drain.
  • Place brisket in an Instant Pot or pressure cooker with garlic, onion, green onion bulbs, Korean radish, black peppercorns, dashida, and 4 cups water.
  • Pressure cook for 40 minutes. If using a stovetop pot instead, simmer on medium heat for 2-3 hours, adding water as needed.
  • Strain the broth through a fine sieve. Remove as much fat as possible — a fat separator works well for a clean broth.
  • Reserve 1/4 cup of the strained broth for the bibim sauce. Set the rest aside (optional: freeze into ice for icy broth later).
  • Once the brisket has cooled, thinly slice it against the grain and set aside.

Prep — Bibim Sauce

  • Remove the core and seeds from the Asian pear (they are bitter).
  • In a blender or food processor, combine: 1/4 cup beef broth, 1/4 Asian pear, 1/4 small onion, 2 garlic cloves, 1 tsp grated ginger, 3-4 tbsp gochugaru, 2 tbsp gochujang, 2 tbsp rice syrup, 1 tbsp sugar, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tsp sesame oil, and 1/2 tsp sea salt.
  • Blend until completely smooth. Taste and adjust — add more gochugaru for heat, sugar for sweetness, or vinegar for tang.
  • The sauce can be made ahead and stored in the fridge for up to 2 weeks — it actually tastes better after a day or two.

Prep — Toppings

  • Thinly slice the cucumber (julienne or use a mandoline for uniform strips).
  • Thinly slice the Asian pear or cut into matchsticks. To prevent browning, place in cold sugar water until ready to assemble, or cut at the very last minute.
  • Hard-boil eggs: bring a pot of water to a boil with a splash of vinegar, gently lower in eggs, set timer for 12 minutes.
  • Prepare an ice bath — immediately transfer eggs to ice water for at least 1 minute, then peel and halve.
  • Using a mortar and pestle (or a ziplock bag and rolling pin), crush sesame seeds coarsely.

Cook — Noodles

  • Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add the naengmyeon noodles and cook for 2.5-3 minutes. Watch closely — the noodles are very porous and cook fast.
  • When almost done, drain immediately and rinse under ice-cold running water. Remove as much starch as possible, but don't leave them under water too long — they'll continue softening even in cold water.
  • Drain well and set aside.

Assemble & Serve

  • Divide the cold noodles between serving bowls — assemble immediately, don't let noodles sit out.
  • Ladle a generous amount of bibim sauce over the noodles in each bowl.
  • Arrange toppings: sliced brisket, cucumber strips, Asian pear slices, halved hard-boiled egg, and a sprinkle of crushed sesame seeds.
  • Optional: pour icy dongchimi broth over the top for extra refreshment (see pro tips).
  • Mix everything together with chopsticks before eating. The noodles, sauce, and toppings should be fully combined.

Pro Tips

  • The bibim sauce is the soul of this dish — make it spicy, tangy, and slightly sweet. Don't be shy with the gochugaru; adjust to your heat preference. The sauce improves after 1-2 days in the fridge, so make it ahead if possible.
  • Chik-naengmyeon noodles (made with arrowroot starch) are the gold standard for texture — chewy and dark. If unavailable, regular buckwheat naengmyeon noodles work fine. Look for them in Korean grocery stores or online.
