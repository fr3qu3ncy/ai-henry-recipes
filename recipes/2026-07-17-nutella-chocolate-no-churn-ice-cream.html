Nutella & Chocolate No-Churn Ice Cream

Why This Recipe

Same no-churn technique as the peach version — clotted cream and condensed milk give an incredibly rich custardy base without a machine. Nutella as the flavour base, white chocolate chunks throughout, dark chocolate shavings on top for sharp contrast.

Ingredients

  • 200g clotted cream (chilled)
  • 395g can condensed milk, at room temperature
  • 200g Nutella
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 100g white chocolate, chopped into small chunks (~0.5-1cm pieces)
  • 50g dark chocolate (70%+), shaved or roughly chopped for topping
  • Optional: pinch of sea salt flakes for finishing

Instructions

Prep the Nutella

  • If the Nutella is thick (on cooler days), warm it gently in a bowl over hot water or 10 seconds in the microwave until pourable and silky — don't overheat, just enough to flow easily. On warmer days it'll be fine as-is.
  • If warmed, let it cool slightly so it doesn't melt the cream when folded in.

Make the Base

  • In a separate bowl, stir the condensed milk, vanilla extract, and salt until smooth.
  • UK clotted cream is thick enough to use straight from the tub — no whipping needed.

Combine

  • Fold the Nutella into the clotted cream first — these are the two thickest ingredients and they'll combine more easily together.
  • Once the Nutella and clotted cream are well folded, gently fold in the condensed milk mixture — just until no white streaks remain.
  • Don't overmix — leave ribbons of hazelnut through the base for a marbled effect.

Add the White Chocolate

  • Fold in the white chocolate chunks gently — just enough to distribute them evenly. They'll soften slightly in the creamy base and give a nice bite when frozen.

Freeze

  • Pour into a loaf tin or shallow container, press cling film directly on the surface to prevent ice crystals.
  • Freeze for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight.

Serve

  • Let sit at room temperature for 5 minutes before scooping.
  • Top generously with the dark chocolate shavings — the contrast between the sweet Nutella-white chocolate base and the bitter dark chocolate on top is the key move here.
  • Finish with a pinch of sea salt if you've got it.

Pro Tips

  • UK clotted cream is thick enough to use straight from the tub — no whipping required.
  • If the Nutella is too thick even after warming, thin it with a teaspoon of hot water — just enough to make it pourable.
  • The white chocolate chunks should be small enough to get in every spoonful but big enough to still feel like a distinct bite. Aim for pea-sized pieces.
  • Dark chocolate shavings work better than chunks on top — use a veg peeler on a bar for proper curls.
