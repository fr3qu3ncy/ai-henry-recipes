Grilled Whole Fish with Lemongrass, Chiles and Coconut

Why This Recipe

Perfect summer grilling: whole fish stuffed with fragrant lemongrass, lime, and herbs, then blasted on the grill and served with a vibrant coconut-chili dipping sauce. Hands-on technique that feels special but comes together in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 4 whole dorade, branzino, or trout (about 1 lb each), cleaned
  • 1/4 tsp fine sea salt, plus more as needed
  • Black pepper, as needed
  • 1 1/2 tbsp coconut oil, softened or melted
  • 3 limes
  • 2 small shallots, peeled
  • 1 bunch cilantro, leaves and stems separated (about 2 cups leaves)
  • 2 small stalks lemongrass, halved lengthwise and cut into 3-inch pieces
  • 1 cup mint leaves, plus more for garnish
  • 1 Thai chile or 1-2 serrano chiles, seeded and coarsely chopped
  • 2 small garlic cloves, grated or minced
  • 1/4 tsp Asian fish sauce, more to taste
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp safflower or grapeseed oil

Instructions

Prep

  • Season the cavities and outside of each fish with salt and pepper. Rub the skin all over with coconut oil.
  • Thinly slice 1 lime and 1 shallot. Cut another lime into wedges and set aside for garnish.
  • Fill each fish cavity with lime slices, shallot slices, a handful of cilantro stems, and the lemongrass pieces.
  • Coarsely chop the remaining shallot and juice the remaining lime.

Make the Sauce

  • In a mini food processor (or by hand), combine the chopped shallot, 1 tbsp lime juice, cilantro leaves, mint, chile, garlic, fish sauce, and 1/4 tsp salt.
  • Pulse until herbs are finely chopped, then add coconut milk and safflower oil. Continue pulsing until a coarse, chunky sauce forms — you want a relish-like texture, not a purée.
  • Taste and adjust with more salt, fish sauce, or lime juice as needed. Sauce can be made up to 4 hours ahead and refrigerated.

Grill

  • Heat the grill to medium-high heat. Make sure grates are clean and well-oiled.
  • Place fish in a grill basket (or directly on the grill if confident). Grill until blistered and just cooked through, 3-4 minutes per side.
  • To check doneness: poke the fish along the spine — the flesh should be tender but still juicy, and it should flake away from the bone.

Serve

  • Transfer fish to plates. Garnish with lime wedges and fresh mint leaves.
  • Serve with the coconut-chili sauce on the side for dipping.
  • Pair with jasmine rice, grilled vegetables, or a simple green salad.

Pro Tips

  • If whole fish feels daunting, this works beautifully with thick fillets (sea bass, cod, or salmon) — just reduce grilling time to 2-3 minutes per side.
  • Oven alternative: roast on a rimmed baking sheet at 425F for 10-15 minutes until cooked through. Same flavors, zero grill needed.
  • Tie the fish cavities shut with kitchen twine before grilling to keep the aromatics inside and prevent the fish from falling apart when flipping.
