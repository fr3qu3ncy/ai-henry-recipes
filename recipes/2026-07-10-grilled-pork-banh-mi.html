Grilled Pork Banh Mi

Why This Recipe

Vietnamese banh mi sandwiches are the ultimate fusion of French technique and Southeast Asian flavors — sweet-savory grilled pork, crunchy pickled slaw, fresh herbs, and a charred crust. Martin's Korean exploration continues into Vietnamese territory, with a recipe that's quick (20 min active cooking), hands-on, and wildly different from anything he's made before.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork loin, sliced 1/4-inch thick
  • 2 large garlic cloves
  • 1 shallot
  • 1 tablespoon dark brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons canola or vegetable oil
  • 4 French rolls or baguettes, halved horizontally
  • 4 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 1 zucchini, sliced thin
  • 6-8 fresh cilantro sprigs
  • 4 jalapeños, sliced
  • For the pickled slaw:
  • 1/2 cup julienned carrots
  • 1/2 cup julienned daikon radish
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 cup distilled white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sweet rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions

Pickled Slaw (make first)

  • In a medium saucepan over high heat, combine 1/4 cup distilled vinegar, 1/4 cup sweet rice vinegar, 1/4 cup sugar, and 1/2 cup water. Bring to a rolling boil.
  • Transfer the hot vinegar mixture to a large shallow bowl.
  • Add the julienned carrots, daikon radish, and 1 teaspoon kosher salt. Stir to combine.
  • Let the vegetables sit in the pickling liquid for 30 minutes, then drain well. The slaw can be made ahead and stored in the fridge.

Marinate the Pork

  • Using a mortar and pestle (or a fine grater), crush the garlic cloves and shallot into a smooth paste.
  • Transfer the paste to a medium bowl. Add brown sugar, fish sauce, mirin, soy sauce, and canola oil. Whisk until the sugar dissolves and the marinade is smooth.
  • Add the sliced pork loin to the bowl, tossing to coat every slice evenly.
  • Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for deeper flavor penetration.

Grill the Pork

  • Thread the marinated pork slices onto skewers (soak wooden skewers in water for 15 minutes first to prevent burning).
  • Preheat your grill or a cast-iron grill pan to medium-high heat.
  • Grill the pork skewers for 5-7 minutes, turning frequently. The pork should develop a slight char on the edges.
  • Check doneness with a meat thermometer — the internal temperature should reach 145°F. Remove from heat.

Assemble the Sandwiches

  • Split the French rolls lengthwise (or use pre-halved rolls).
  • Spread about 1 tablespoon each of softened butter and mayonnaise on the cut side of each half.
  • Layer the grilled pork slices on the bottom half of each roll.
  • Top with the pickled slaw, thinly sliced zucchini, fresh cilantro sprigs, and sliced jalapeños.
  • Close the sandwich and serve immediately.

Pro Tips

  • No mortar and pestle? Pulse the garlic and shallot in a food processor, or grate them on a fine microplane — you want a smooth paste, not chunks.
  • The pickled slaw can be made a day ahead — the flavors actually improve overnight. Store in a sealed jar in the fridge for up to a week.
  • No grill? A cast-iron skillet or grill pan works perfectly — just get it smoking hot before adding the pork.
