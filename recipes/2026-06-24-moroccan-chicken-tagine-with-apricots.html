Moroccan Chicken Tagine with Apricots

Why This Recipe

North African cooking is a flavor adventure Martin hasn't explored yet — warm spices, sweet apricots, and slow-braised chicken in one pot. Perfectly seasonal too, with summer coriander and fresh ingredients brightening the rich sauce. Hands-off simmering means minimal active work for maximum reward.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried apricots, halved
  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (220g/7oz each)
  • 3/4 tsp salt, for seasoning chicken
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 brown onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely minced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 400g can crushed tomatoes
  • 400g can chickpeas, drained
  • 1 tbsp preserved lemon skin, finely minced
  • 2 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken stock
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3/4 tsp ground cumin
  • 3/4 tsp ground ginger
  • 3/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp ground allspice
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/8 tsp ground clove
  • Couscous, to serve
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander, roughly chopped
  • 1/3 cup slivered almonds, lightly toasted (optional garnish)

Instructions

Prep

  • Soak dried apricots in a bowl of boiling water for 30 minutes, then drain. This plumps them up and softens them.
  • Sprinkle both sides of the chicken thighs with salt.
  • Toast slivered almonds in a dry pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until golden, if using. Set aside.

Brown the Chicken

  • Heat olive oil in a large, deep skillet or heavy-bottomed pot over high heat.
  • Place chicken skin-side down and cook for 8-10 minutes until deeply golden and crisp. Do not move the chicken — let the skin render.
  • Turn chicken over and cook the flesh side for 1 minute, then remove to a plate.

Build the Sauce

  • Discard all but 2 tablespoons of fat from the skillet. Reduce heat to medium.
  • Add sliced onions and cook for 3 minutes until softened.
  • Add minced garlic, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  • Add the spice blend (cumin, ginger, black pepper, allspice, cinnamon, coriander, clove) and stir for 30 seconds until the spices bloom and smell intensely aromatic.
  • Add the cinnamon stick, crushed tomatoes, chickpeas, plumped apricots, chicken stock, preserved lemon, and salt. Stir well to combine.

Simmer

  • Nestle the chicken thighs on top of the sauce, skin-side up.
  • Bring the liquid to a simmer, then cover with a lid. Cook for 5 minutes, adjusting heat so the liquid simmers gently (not boiling rapidly, or the base may catch).
  • Remove the lid and continue cooking uncovered for 20 minutes until the sauce has thickened and reduced, and the chicken internal temperature reaches at least 70°C/158°F. The sauce should coat the back of a spoon.

Serve

  • Remove from heat and let rest for 5 minutes — this lets the sauce settle and the chicken finish cooking gently.
  • Serve over fluffy couscous, spooning plenty of sauce, chickpeas, and apricots around each portion.
  • Sprinkle with fresh coriander and toasted almonds for garnish.

Pro Tips

  • The spice blend (cumin, ginger, allspice, cinnamon, coriander, clove) is a simplified Ras el hanout — North Africa's signature spice mix. Bloom the spices in the hot oil for 30 seconds before adding liquid to unlock their full aroma.
  • If you can't find preserved lemon, substitute with 2 tbsp fresh lemon zest plus 1 tbsp lemon juice — it won't be quite the same depth but works well.
  • This tagine actually tastes better the next day as the flavors meld. Make it ahead and reheat gently for an even richer result.
