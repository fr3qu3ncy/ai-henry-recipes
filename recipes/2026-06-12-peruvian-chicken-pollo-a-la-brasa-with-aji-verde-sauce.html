Peruvian Chicken (Pollo a la Brasa) with Aji Verde Sauce

Why This Recipe

A completely new cuisine for you — Peruvian. Pollo a la Brasa is Peru's iconic roast chicken, marinated in aji amarillo and aji panca (two Peruvian chilli pastes that are game-changers to keep in the pantry), then roasted until deeply golden. Served with a creamy, tangy aji verde green sauce that's addictive. The marinade technique is simple, the flavour is extraordinary, and it introduces you to ingredients that open up a whole new world of cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (1.5-1.8kg), cut into 8 pieces (or 6 bone-in chicken thighs as shortcut)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp aji amarillo paste (see pro tips for UK sourcing / substitute)
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp aji panca paste (or extra paprika + pinch of smoked paprika)
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Juice of 1 small lime
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, for drizzling
  • For the Aji Verde (Green) Sauce:
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp aji amarillo paste (or 3-4 jalapeños, deseeded and pureed)
  • 1 shallot, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (or lime juice)
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (or queso blanco / grated parmesan)
  • 1.5 cups finely chopped fresh cilantro

Instructions

Marinate

  • Pat the chicken pieces dry with paper towels and place in a large bowl.
  • In a small bowl, whisk together the minced garlic, soy sauce, aji amarillo paste, honey, aji panca paste, paprika, oregano, coriander, cumin, lime juice, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  • Pour the marinade over the chicken and rub it all over — get it under the skin where possible for maximum flavour penetration.
  • Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Overnight is even better — the longer the marinade sits, the deeper the flavour.

Roast

  • Preheat the oven to 190°C / 375°F.
  • Remove the chicken from the marinade and arrange skin-side up in a large roasting tin or cast iron skillet. You can discard the used marinade or reserve it for deglazing.
  • Drizzle generously with olive oil.
  • Roast for 40-45 minutes until the skin is deeply golden brown and the chicken is cooked through. The internal temperature should reach 74°C / 165°F at the thickest part. If using bone-in thighs, check at 35 minutes — they may be done sooner.
  • If the skin isn't as golden as you'd like, turn on the grill/broiler for the last 2-3 minutes, watching carefully.

Make the Aji Verde Sauce

  • While the chicken roasts, add the mayonnaise, aji amarillo paste, minced shallot, minced garlic, vinegar, and lemon juice to a food processor or blender.
  • Blend until smooth and creamy.
  • Add the crumbled feta and chopped cilantro. Process for another 30-60 seconds until well combined but still slightly textured.
  • Taste and adjust — add more lemon juice for brightness, more salt if needed. The sauce should be creamy, tangy, and gently spicy.
  • Set aside at room temperature while the chicken rests.

Rest and Serve

  • Remove the chicken from the oven and tent loosely with foil. Let rest for 10 minutes — this keeps the juices inside.
  • Arrange the chicken pieces on a serving platter.
  • Serve with the aji verde sauce in a bowl on the side for dipping, or spooned over the chicken.
  • Pairs brilliantly with Peruvian yellow rice, boiled potatoes, or just fries. A simple green salad works too.

Pro Tips

  • Aji amarillo paste is the soul of Peruvian cooking — it's a fruity, medium-heat chilli paste that colours everything a beautiful golden yellow. You can order it online (Amazon UK stocks it) or find it in Latin American specialty shops. In a pinch, blend 2-3 yellow/orange bell peppers with 1 habanero or Scotch bonnet and a splash of vinegar — it won't be identical but gets you close.
  • Aji panca is a smokier, milder Peruvian chilli paste. If you can't find it, substitute with 1 tsp smoked paprika + a pinch of regular paprika. It won't be authentic but the dish will still be delicious.
  • The aji verde sauce keeps in the fridge for up to 5 days — make extra and use it as a dip for fries, a spread for sandwiches, or a dressing for grilled fish.
  • For the crispiest skin, pat the chicken very dry before roasting and don't overcrowd the pan — the skin needs space to crisp, not steam.
