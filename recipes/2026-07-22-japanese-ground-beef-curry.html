Japanese Ground Beef Curry

Why This Recipe

Japanese curry (kare) is a comforting, deeply flavorful dish that's all the rage right now — trending on NYT Cooking as one of the most popular recipes of 2026. Unlike Indian curry, it uses a sweet-savory roux-based sauce that's mellow, rich, and approachable. Perfect introduction to Japanese home cooking.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons sunflower, safflower or canola oil
  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 large garlic clove, minced
  • 1 (1-inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and minced
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and chopped into 1/3-inch pieces
  • 1 large russet potato, peeled and chopped into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 (3.2-ounce) package Japanese curry roux bricks (preferably medium-hot S&B Golden Curry)
  • 3/4 cup frozen peas
  • Cooked rice, udon, or lo mein noodles, for serving

Instructions

Brown the Beef

  • Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the oil and when it shimmers, add the ground beef, a generous pinch of salt and a couple pinches of ground pepper.
  • Using a spatula, break up the beef and spread it into one layer on the skillet. Let cook undisturbed until browned on the bottom, about 3 minutes.

Cook the Aromatics

  • Stir and push the beef to one side of the skillet. Add the chopped onion to the other side and season lightly with salt.
  • Let cook, stirring occasionally, about 2 minutes. Add the garlic and ginger, stir all ingredients to combine and continue to cook about 3 minutes more.

Simmer the Curry

  • Stir in the carrots and potato and cook until the edges begin to soften, about 4 minutes.
  • Add the curry roux bricks and 2 cups of water. Stir, breaking up the bricks until they melt into a sauce and start bubbling.
  • Cover, lower heat to low, and let simmer, stirring occasionally, until the liquid has reduced into a rich, viscous sauce, about 15 minutes.
  • If the sauce has reduced too much (drying at the bottom), gradually add up to 1/2 cup water, a few tablespoons at a time. The sauce should be glossy and saucy, not gloopy or congealed.

Finish and Serve

  • Add the frozen peas and cook until bright green, 3 to 5 minutes.
  • Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Serve over cooked rice, udon, or lo mein noodles.
  • Leftovers keep refrigerated for up to 3 days and taste even better the next day.

Pro Tips

  • Japanese curry roux bricks are available at Asian grocery stores and online — S&B Golden Curry (medium-hot) is the gold standard. The bricks contain the roux, spices, and thickener all in one — no need for separate curry powder or flour.
  • For a richer curry, replace 1/2 cup of the water with milk or add a tablespoon of honey and a splash of soy sauce at the end. This is a classic Japanese home-cook trick.
