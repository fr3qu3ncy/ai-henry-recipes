Miso Butter Ramen with Soft-Boiled Egg

Why This Recipe

White miso and brown butter create an incredibly rich, umami-packed broth that's surprisingly quick. The technique of browning butter before adding miso unlocks nutty depth you won't find in standard ramen recipes. Perfect for a Friday night when you want something restaurant-quality without the fuss.

Ingredients

  • 200g fresh ramen noodles or thick egg noodles
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 400ml chicken or vegetable stock
  • 100ml water
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 soft-boiled eggs (6.5 minutes)
  • 1 sheet nori, cut into strips
  • Sesame seeds for garnish
  • Chili oil to serve (optional)

Instructions

Prep

  • Soft-boil eggs: Lower gently into simmering water, time 6 minutes 30 seconds, then transfer to ice water. Peel when cool.
  • Mince garlic and grate ginger.
  • Slice spring onions thinly on the diagonal.
  • Cut nori sheet into 4 thin strips.

Broth

  • Melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Watch carefully — it will foam, then subside, then turn amber and smell nutty (about 2-3 minutes). Do not walk away.
  • Remove from heat briefly. Whisk in miso paste until smooth.
  • Return to low heat. Add garlic and ginger, cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  • Pour in stock and water, whisk to dissolve miso fully. Add soy sauce and mirin.
  • Simmer gently for 5 minutes. Do not boil vigorously — it breaks down the miso.

Noodles

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to the boil. Cook ramen noodles according to package (usually 2-3 minutes for fresh).
  • Drain noodles well. Divide between 2 warm bowls.

Serve

  • Ladle hot broth over noodles.
  • Halve soft-boiled eggs and place on top.
  • Garnish with spring onions, nori strips, and sesame seeds.
  • Add a drizzle of chili oil if you want heat.

Pro Tips

  • The key is browning the butter slowly — the nutty compounds (methylpyrazines) it develops pair perfectly with miso's glutamates for maximum umami impact.
  • If you can't find fresh ramen, thick spaghetti works fine. Just reduce cook time by 1 minute and reserve some pasta water to thin the broth.
