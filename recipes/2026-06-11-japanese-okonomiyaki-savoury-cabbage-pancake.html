Japanese Okonomiyaki (Savoury Cabbage Pancake)

Why This Recipe

A completely new technique: Japanese savoury pancakes loaded with shredded cabbage and pork belly, topped with dancing bonito flakes. 30 minutes, uses cheap ingredients, and introduces pantry staples (bonito flakes, nori, bouillon) that open up a whole world of Japanese home cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • 60g plain flour
  • 1 tsp vegetable bouillon powder
  • 100ml water
  • 200g green cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 100g cooked pork belly, thinly sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 1½ tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1½ tbsp ketchup
  • ½ tbsp caster sugar
  • 2-4 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 green nori (seaweed) sheet, shredded
  • 3 spring onions, finely sliced
  • Katsuobushi (bonito flakes), to serve
  • Black pepper, to season

Instructions

Make the Batter

  • Crack the egg into a large bowl. Add the flour, bouillon powder, and 100ml water. Whisk until completely smooth — no lumps.
  • Stir in the finely shredded cabbage. Season generously with black pepper. The batter should be thick but pourable, like a thick pancake mix.

Cook the Pork

  • Heat the vegetable oil in a frying pan or skillet (around 19cm) over medium heat.
  • Add the sliced pork belly. Fry for 4-5 minutes until golden and the fat has rendered — the pork should be crispy at the edges.
  • Do not remove the pork — you'll build the pancake right on top of it in the same pan.

Fry the Okonomiyaki

  • Pour the cabbage batter over the pork belly in the pan. Spread it into an even layer, about 2cm thick.
  • Fry undisturbed for 5 minutes until the base is dark golden brown and the edges look set. The cabbage should be semi-cooked through.
  • Flip the pancake — this is the moment of truth. Use a large spatula, or slide it onto a dinner plate, invert the plate over the pan, then flip it back. The cooked side should be a rich, even brown.
  • Cook the second side for 5 minutes until golden and the centre is cooked through. Press gently — it should feel firm, not soggy.

Make the Sauce

  • While the pancake cooks, whisk together the Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, and caster sugar in a small bowl.
  • The sugar should dissolve — if the sauce seems thick, add a teaspoon of water to loosen it. Season lightly with pepper.

Assemble and Serve

  • Transfer the okonomiyaki to a serving plate.
  • Drizzle generously with the homemade sauce, then add lines of mayonnaise across the top.
  • Scatter over the shredded nori, sliced spring onions, and a generous handful of bonito flakes.
  • Serve immediately — the bonito flakes will 'dance' from the residual heat of the pancake. It's a proper Japanese restaurant trick.

Pro Tips

  • The flip is the trickiest part. If you're nervous, use the plate method: slide the pancake onto a flat plate, turn the plate over the pan, then slide it back in cooked-side-up. A non-stick pan makes this much easier.
  • For a proper upgrade, buy okonomiyaki sauce from the Asian aisle of your supermarket — it's a thick, sweet-savoury condiment that tastes nothing like the Worcestershire+ketchup substitute. Keep a bottle in the cupboard; it lasts for months and opens up loads of Japanese recipes.
  • Swap the pork belly for prawns, chicken strips, or mushrooms — okonomiyaki means 'grilled as you like it', so it's designed to be improvised with whatever you have.
