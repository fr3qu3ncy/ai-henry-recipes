Dak Galbi (Korean Spicy Chicken Stir Fry)

Why This Recipe

Korean dak galbi is a fiery one-pan stir-fry that packs gochujang-spiked chicken, sweet potato, rice cakes, and cabbage into a single sizzling skillet. Perfect for summer when you want bold flavour without standing over the stove.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken thigh fillets, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1/2 sweet potato, cut into long thick sticks (like chips)
  • 1/2 carrot, diagonally sliced
  • 1/4 cabbage, shredded
  • 10 leaves Korean perilla, thinly sliced (or substitute with fresh basil)
  • 18 pieces Korean rice cakes (tteok), soaked in warm water for 10 mins
  • 2-3 Tbsp cooking oil (rice bran or neutral oil)
  • 3 Tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 Tbsp rice wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 Tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp raw sugar
  • 1 Tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 tsp Korean curry powder (or regular curry powder)
  • 1/2 onion, grated or minced
  • Ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions

Prep

  • Combine all marinade ingredients (gochujang, rice wine, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, garlic, ginger, curry powder, onion, black pepper) in a bowl and whisk until smooth.
  • Pour the marinade over the chopped chicken thighs and mix well to coat evenly.
  • Marinate for at least 30 minutes (4+ hours or overnight for best flavour).
  • While chicken marinates, prep vegetables: cut sweet potato into thick sticks, slice carrot diagonally, shred cabbage, and thinly slice perilla leaves (or basil).
  • If using pre-packaged rice cakes, separate them and soak in warm water for 10 minutes.

Cook

  • Preheat a large skillet or cast-iron pan over medium-high heat.
  • Add 2-3 Tbsp cooking oil to the pan once hot.
  • Add all vegetables and rice cakes to the skillet, spreading them in an even layer.
  • Place the marinated chicken on top of the vegetables.
  • Cook on medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes to sear the chicken.
  • Reduce heat to medium-low and continue cooking for 10-15 minutes until chicken is fully cooked and vegetables are tender.
  • Stir frequently during cooking to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
  • Cover with a lid partway through to speed up cooking if needed.

Serve

  • Transfer directly from the skillet to the table — dak galbi is traditionally served in the cooking pan.
  • Serve with steamed white rice on the side.
  • Add Korean side dishes (banchan) like kimchi, pickled radish, or seasoned spinach if available.

Pro Tips

  • Marinate overnight for the deepest flavour — the gochujang and curry powder penetrate the chicken beautifully with time.
  • Cant find Korean perilla? Fresh basil is the closest substitute and works brilliantly. Skip the rice cakes if unavailable — the dish still works without them, just add extra sweet potato.
  • If you like it extra saucy, add 2-3 Tbsp water during cooking to create a glossy glaze that clings to everything.
