Korean Gochujang Glazed Chicken Thighs

Why This Recipe

Gochujang (Korean fermented chili paste) gives these thighs a deep, complex heat that's sweet, smoky, and garlicky all at once. The glaze caramelises beautifully on the skin while the meat stays juicy. A proper weeknight winner that feels like a treat.

Ingredients

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 3 tbsp gochujang paste
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 red pepper, sliced into strips
  • 2 spring onions, sliced
  • 100g baby spinach
  • Sesame seeds for garnish
  • Steamed rice to serve

Instructions

Prep

  • Mix gochujang, soy sauce, honey, rice wine vinegar, garlic, and ginger in a small bowl to make the glaze.
  • Pat chicken thighs dry with kitchen paper. This ensures crispy skin.
  • Slice red pepper into thin strips.

Cook

  • Heat a large oven-proof frying pan over medium-high heat. Add a drizzle of oil.
  • Place chicken thighs skin-side down. Cook 6-7 minutes without moving them until skin is deep golden and crispy.
  • Flip thighs. Brush generously with gochujang glaze. Cook 3 more minutes.
  • Transfer pan to oven at 200°C for 12-15 minutes until chicken is cooked through and glaze is bubbling.
  • In the last 5 minutes, add red pepper strips to the pan around the chicken.

Finish

  • Remove from oven. Drizzle with sesame oil.
  • Wilt baby spinach over the chicken for 30 seconds using residual heat.
  • Garnish with spring onions and sesame seeds.

Serve

  • Serve immediately over steamed rice.
  • Spoon any pan juices and glaze over the top.

Pro Tips

  • For extra crispy skin, score the skin diagonally 3-4 times before cooking. This renders fat faster and prevents curling.
  • If your gochujang is particularly thick, thin the glaze with a tablespoon of warm water. It should coat the back of a spoon.
