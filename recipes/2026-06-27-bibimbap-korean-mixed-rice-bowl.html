Bibimbap (Korean Mixed Rice Bowl)

Why This Recipe

The crown jewel of Korean home cooking — a beautifully arranged rice bowl layered with individually seasoned vegetables, marinated beef, and a fried egg, all brought together with gochujang sauce. Perfect for your Korean cuisine exploration, and a proper technique dish that feels special but comes together in about 70 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups cooked short-grain white rice (preferably steamed sushi rice)
  • 250g beef tenderloin or thick steak, very finely sliced across the grain
  • 1/4 green apple, finely grated (with juices)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced (divided)
  • 1 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce (for beef marinade)
  • 1 tbsp honey or brown sugar (for beef marinade)
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame oil (for beef marinade)
  • 2 large carrots, cut into 5cm x 0.5cm batons
  • 2 large zucchini, cut into 5cm x 0.5cm batons
  • 1 bunch spinach, cut into 5cm lengths
  • 8 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 4 cups bean sprouts
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tsp sesame seeds, toasted
  • 8 tsp vegetable oil, divided
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1.5 tsp soy sauce (for vegetables)
  • 1/4 tsp fish sauce (or extra soy sauce)
  • 1/4 tsp white sugar (for vegetables)
  • For the gochujang sauce: 4 tbsp gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • For the gochujang sauce: 2 tbsp mirin
  • For the gochujang sauce: 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • For the gochujang sauce: 1.5 tsp soy sauce
  • For the gochujang sauce: 3 tsp white sugar
  • For the gochujang sauce: 1 garlic clove, finely grated
  • For the gochujang sauce: 2.5 tsp toasted sesame oil

Instructions

Make the Gochujang Sauce

  • In a small bowl, combine gochujang paste, mirin, rice vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, grated garlic, and sesame oil.
  • Whisk until smooth and well combined. The sauce should be glossy and slightly thick. Set aside.
  • Taste and adjust — add more sugar for sweetness, more vinegar for tang, or more gochujang for heat.

Marinate the Beef

  • Slice the beef very thinly across the grain — this is crucial for tenderness.
  • In a bowl, combine the sliced beef with grated green apple (with its juices), 2 minced garlic cloves, ginger, soy sauce, honey, and 2 tsp sesame oil.
  • Toss thoroughly to coat every slice. The apple's enzymes help tenderize the meat naturally.
  • Let marinate for at least 15 minutes at room temperature while you prepare the vegetables.

Prepare the Vegetables

  • Soak the dried shiitake mushrooms in hot water for 20 minutes until softened. Squeeze out excess water, chop roughly, and reserve the soaking liquid.
  • Cut carrots and zucchini into uniform 5cm x 0.5cm batons so they cook evenly.
  • Cut spinach into 5cm lengths. Rinse bean sprouts thoroughly.
  • Have all vegetables prepped and ready — each one cooks separately and quickly.

Cook the Vegetables (Separately)

  • Heat 1 tsp vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add carrots with a pinch of salt and 1/4 tsp soy sauce. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until just tender-crisp. Remove and set aside.
  • Add 1 tsp oil to the pan. Add zucchini batons with a pinch of salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes until lightly charred at the edges. Remove and set aside.
  • Add 1 tsp oil. Add spinach and a splash of the reserved mushroom soaking liquid. Wilt for 1 minute until just cooked. Squeeze out excess liquid and set aside.
  • Add 1 tsp oil. Add bean sprouts, 1/4 tsp fish sauce, and 1/4 tsp sugar. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until tender but still crisp. Remove and set aside.
  • Add 1 tsp oil. Add chopped shiitake mushrooms and cook for 2-3 minutes until golden. Remove and set aside.

Cook the Beef

  • Heat 1 tsp vegetable oil in the skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  • Add the marinated beef in a single layer — work in batches if your pan is small. Do not crowd.
  • Sear for 1-2 minutes per side until browned and just cooked through. The beef should be pink inside, not grey.
  • Remove immediately to a plate. Overcooking makes it tough.

Fry the Eggs

  • Heat 1 tsp oil in a small frying pan over medium heat.
  • Crack an egg into the pan. Fry for 2-3 minutes until the white is set but the yolk is still runny.
  • Repeat for all 4 eggs. Keep them warm on a plate.
  • For the classic bibimbap look, fry them sunny-side up with a nice runny yolk.

Assemble

  • Divide the hot steamed rice among 4 bowls, pressing it down gently to create a flat base.
  • Arrange the cooked vegetables in neat rows or sections on top of the rice — carrots, zucchini, spinach, bean sprouts, mushrooms.
  • Place the seared beef on top of the vegetables.
  • Top each bowl with a fried egg, yolk-side up.
  • Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds.
  • Serve with the gochujang sauce on the side, or drizzle a generous spoonful over each bowl.

Pro Tips

  • The green apple in the beef marinade is a Korean secret — its natural enzymes (bromelain) tenderize the meat better than any mechanical method, and the subtle sweetness balances the soy and sesame.
  • Cook each vegetable separately — this is what makes bibimbap special. Each ingredient retains its distinct texture and flavor rather than becoming a mushy stir-fry. The extra 10 minutes is absolutely worth it.
  • Toast your own sesame seeds — dry-fry them in a small pan for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and lightly golden. The flavor difference over pre-toasted seeds is remarkable.
  • If you want the authentic stone bowl (dolsot) experience, preheat a heatproof bowl in the oven at 200°C for 10 minutes, then assemble the bibimbap in it. The rice will caramelize at the bottom, creating a crispy layer called nurungji.
