Seared Salmon with Avocado-Lime Salsa, Warm Spinach & Chilli Beans

Why This Recipe

Vietnamese-inspired plate that's bright, fresh, and layered with gentle heat. Uses seasonal avocado and radish with crispy-skinned salmon — restaurant quality in 25 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (skin-on), patted dry
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 1 tbsp rapeseed or olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 200g fine beans, trimmed, cut into 4cm lengths
  • 100g baby spinach
  • 2 spring onions, white parts only, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 rocket chilli, finely sliced
  • 1 red chilli, deseeded, finely sliced
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (or soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 ripe avocados, halved, stoned, diced
  • 2 radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1 spring onion, finely sliced (green parts)
  • 1 tbsp fresh mint, torn
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • ½ lime, cut into wedges for serving
  • ½ lemon, juice
  • 1 small mango, diced (optional)
  • Pinch of sea salt flakes
  • 1 ball mozzarella, torn into pieces

Instructions

Prep

  • Pat salmon dry, season generously with salt and pepper.
  • Dice avocados, toss immediately with lime juice to prevent browning.
  • Slice radishes into thin rounds.
  • Slice both chillies — rocket chilli rings for the beans, red chilli strips for the salsa.
  • Chop herbs, toast sesame seeds in a dry pan for 1 minute until fragrant.
  • If using mango, peel and dice.

Sear the salmon

  • Heat oil and butter in a large pan over medium-high.
  • Place salmon skin-side down, press gently for 10 seconds so the skin lays flat.
  • Cook 4-5 minutes until skin is crispy, flip, cook 2-3 minutes more.
  • Remove to a plate, tent loosely with foil, rest.

Cook the beans & spinach

  • In the same pan (keep the salmon fond!), add olive oil.
  • Sauté white spring onion parts and garlic for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  • Add both chillies, cook 30 seconds more — the heat blooms into the oil.
  • Toss in fine beans, cook 2-3 minutes until tender-crisp.
  • Add fish sauce, season with salt and pepper.
  • Toss in baby spinach, cook just until wilted (30 seconds).
  • Remove from heat.

Build the salsa

  • In a bowl, combine diced avocado, radish slices, mint, coriander, and mango if using.
  • Sprinkle with sesame seeds and sea salt flakes.
  • Dress with lime juice and lemon juice, fold gently — don't mash the avocado.

Plate

  • Spoon warm spinach and beans as the base.
  • Place salmon fillet on top.
  • Ladle salsa generously over everything.
  • Scatter cheese — mozzarella will soften from the heat, feta or goat cheese will add tang.
  • Final squeeze of lime, extra rocket chilli rings if you want more heat.

Pro Tips

  • Press the salmon gently for 10 seconds when it hits the pan — prevents curling and ensures even searing.
  • Bloom the chillies in hot oil before adding beans — it distributes heat evenly rather than in hot spots.
  • Dress avocado with acid immediately after cutting to stop oxidation.
  • If mozzarella feels too mild, swap for feta or goat cheese for a sharper tang that cuts through the richness.
  • Mango is optional but highly recommended — the sweetness balances the chilli heat beautifully.
  • Don't overcook the spinach — 30 seconds is enough. It'll keep wilting from residual heat.
