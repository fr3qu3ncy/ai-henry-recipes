Lomo Saltado (Peruvian Beef Stir Fry)

Why This Recipe

Lomo Saltado is a brilliant example of Chifa cuisine — the Chinese-Peruvian fusion born from 19th century Chinese immigration to Peru. Thinly seared beef, blistered onions, and juicy tomatoes tossed in a savory soy-aji sauce, served with both rice AND fries. It's quick (30 min), packed with umami, and a technique Martin hasn't tried yet.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound flank steak (or sirloin/skirt steak), cut into thin slices against the grain
  • 5 tablespoons low sodium soy sauce, divided
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced or grated, divided
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Aji Amarillo paste (or sub: blend 1/4 serrano + 1/4 yellow bell pepper)
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 red onion, sliced into 1/2 inch wedges
  • 3 Roma tomatoes, seeds removed and sliced into 1/2 inch wedges
  • Sliced scallions and chopped cilantro, for garnish
  • Vegetable oil, for cooking
  • Cooked French fries and white rice, for serving

Instructions

Prep

  • Cut the flank steak into thin slices across the grain — this shortens muscle fibers for maximum tenderness.
  • For easier slicing, place the steak in the freezer for 10-15 minutes to firm it up before cutting.
  • Mince the garlic. Slice the red onion into 1/2 inch wedges. Core and seed the Roma tomatoes, then cut into 1/2 inch wedges.
  • Have everything mise en place ready — this dish cooks fast and you don't want to be chopping while the pan is hot.

Marinate

  • Place the sliced beef in a bowl. Add 1 tablespoon soy sauce and roughly half the minced garlic. Toss to coat.
  • Let the beef marinate for 5-7 minutes while you prepare the sauce. (For tougher cuts, add 1/4 teaspoon baking soda and marinate 30 minutes.)

Make the Sauce

  • In a small bowl or jar, combine the remaining 4 tablespoons soy sauce, oyster sauce, red wine vinegar, Aji Amarillo paste, and cornstarch.
  • Whisk or shake until smooth and set aside.

Sear the Beef

  • Heat about 1 tablespoon of oil in a cast iron skillet or wok over high heat until the oil is shimmering.
  • Add the beef in batches — don't crowd the pan. Sear for 1-2 minutes, flipping to cook evenly on both sides. You want a deep sear, not steaming.
  • Remove the beef to a clean plate as each batch is done.

Stir-Fry Vegetables

  • Add another tablespoon of oil to the hot pan if it's dry.
  • Toss in the red onion wedges. Let them cook for 1 minute until they start to soften and char at the edges.
  • Add the remaining garlic and the tomato wedges. Sauté for another 1 minute — the tomatoes should warm through but stay firm.

Combine & Serve

  • Return the seared beef to the pan. Drizzle the prepared sauce over everything and toss to coat evenly.
  • If you prefer fries mixed into the stir-fry, add them now. Otherwise, serve them crispy on the side.
  • Top with sliced scallions and chopped cilantro.
  • Serve immediately over steamed white rice with French fries on the side.

Pro Tips

  • Freeze the steak for 10-15 minutes before slicing — it firms up enough to cut paper-thin slices easily, which is key for a proper sear.
  • If you can't find Aji Amarillo paste, blend 1/4 serrano pepper with 1/4 yellow bell pepper for a close approximation of that mild Peruvian heat and fruity sweetness.
  • The baking soda trick for tougher cuts works by raising the meat's pH, preventing proteins from bonding too tightly — it keeps the beef tender even after high-heat searing.
