Bibim Guksu (Korean Cold Spicy Noodles)

Why This Recipe

Bibim guksu is Korea's answer to beating the summer heat — cold, chewy noodles tossed in a fiery gochujang sauce and loaded with crisp vegetables. Perfect for July, and a great addition to your Korean cooking experiments.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz (225g) thin wheat noodles (somyeon)
  • 1/2 cup chopped kimchi
  • 2 lettuce leaves, shredded
  • 1/2 English cucumber, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 perilla leaves (kkaennip), sliced thin (optional — skip if you can't find them)
  • 1 hard or soft boiled egg, sliced in half
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds, coarsely crushed
  • 2 tbsp Korean chili paste (gochujang)
  • 1 tbsp Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar (or white vinegar)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions

Prep

  • Make the bibim sauce: in a medium bowl, whisk together gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and sesame oil until smooth. Set aside.
  • Crush the toasted sesame seeds in a mortar and pestle (or press with the back of a spoon in a small bowl) until coarsely ground. Set aside.
  • Prepare toppings: shred lettuce, slice cucumber into thin matchsticks, slice perilla leaves thin (if using), and hard-boil an egg (6-7 minutes for hard, 6.5 for jammy).

Cook

  • Bring a medium pot of water to a rolling boil. Add the somyeon noodles and cook according to package directions (usually 3-5 minutes).
  • For maximum chewiness: when the water returns to a full boil after adding noodles, pour in 1/2 cup cold water. Repeat this 2-3 more times each time the water re-boils.
  • Drain noodles in a colander and rinse thoroughly under cold running water while tossing with your hands. This stops cooking, removes excess starch, and makes the noodles extra chewy.
  • Shake the colander well to drain completely — wet noodles dilute the sauce.

Assemble

  • Transfer the cold, drained noodles to a large mixing bowl. Pour the bibim sauce over the noodles.
  • Toss vigorously with your hands or kitchen tongs until every strand is evenly coated in the red sauce.
  • Divide noodles between 2 serving bowls.
  • Arrange toppings: pile chopped kimchi, shredded lettuce, cucumber strips, and perilla leaves on top. Place the sliced egg on one side.
  • Sprinkle generously with crushed toasted sesame seeds. Serve immediately while noodles are still cold.

Pro Tips

  • The cold-water shock during boiling is the secret to restaurant-quality chewy noodles — don't skip it. It gelatinizes the starch differently, giving that signature bouncy texture.
  • Make the bibim sauce ahead and keep it in the fridge for up to a week (add fresh garlic to each batch rather than storing it in the sauce — it turns bitter). Double or triple the sauce recipe for quick weeknight meals.
