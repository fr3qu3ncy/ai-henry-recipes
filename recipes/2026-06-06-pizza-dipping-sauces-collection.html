Why This Recipe

A great dipping sauce elevates homemade pizza from nice to proper thing. These range from classic to adventurous — all quick to make, all pair brilliantly with pizza. Pick one (or three) and keep them in a ramekin on the side.

Instructions

Garlic Aioli

  • Crush 2 cloves garlic and mix with 4 tbsp mayonnaise, 1 tsp lemon juice, and a splash of olive oil.
  • Whisk everything together. Done in 2 minutes. Best with: everything — the universal dip.

Chimichurri

  • Blend or finely chop: 1 large handful fresh parsley, 2 cloves garlic, 2 tbsp red wine vinegar, 80ml olive oil, 1 tsp chilli flakes, pinch of salt.
  • Let it sit 10 minutes for flavours to meld. Best with: sausage pizza, fennel, lemon.

Hot Honey

  • Mix 3 tbsp honey with 1-2 tsp chilli flakes. Let sit 10 minutes.
  • Drizzle over pizza or dip. Add more flakes if you want it hotter. Best with: Margherita, anything cheesy.

Classic Pesto

  • Blend: large handful fresh basil, 30g pine nuts (or walnuts), 1 clove garlic, 40g grated parmesan, 60ml olive oil.
  • Blend until smooth, drizzle oil in slowly. Season with salt. Best with: white pizza, burrata, mozzarella-heavy.

Intense Marinara Dip

  • Cook down: 400g tin chopped tomatoes, 3 cloves crushed garlic, 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp sugar, fresh basil, salt and black pepper.
  • Cook 20+ minutes until thick and intense. More garlic than you think. Cool before dipping. Best with: any pizza.

Harissa Yogurt

  • Whisk together: 100g Greek yogurt, 1-2 tbsp harissa paste, 1 tsp lemon juice.
  • Creamy with a slow-building heat. Surprisingly brilliant with pizza. Best with: spiced toppings, Middle Eastern twist.

Truffle Aioli

  • Mix: 4 tbsp mayonnaise, 1 tsp truffle oil, 1 clove crushed garlic.
  • Three ingredients. Fancy but effortless. Best with: mushrooms, truffle oil pizza.

Balsamic Glaze

  • Use good quality balsamic vinegar reduction — buy it or reduce balsamic vinegar in a pan until syrupy.
  • Thick, sweet-tart. Best with: burrata, fresh tomatoes, anything cheesy.

Buffalo Sauce

  • Mix: 3 tbsp Frank's RedHot (or hot sauce), 2 tbsp melted butter, splash of white vinegar.
  • Classic American heat. Best with: chicken pizza, American-style.

Sriracha Mayo

  • Whisk: 4 tbsp mayonnaise, 1-2 tbsp sriracha, squeeze of lime.
  • Adjust sriracha to your heat preference. Best with: Asian-inspired toppings.

Pro Tips

  • Make 2-3 sauces at once and keep them in small ramekins on the table for a proper pizza night experience.
  • Chimichurri and pesto both keep well in the fridge for a week — make extra.
  • Hot honey doubles as a drizzle over the pizza itself, not just a dip.
  • The marinara dip should be cooked longer and thicker than what you'd put on pizza — it needs to stand up to repeated dipping.
