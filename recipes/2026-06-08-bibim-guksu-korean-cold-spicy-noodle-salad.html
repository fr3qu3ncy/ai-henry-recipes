Bibim Guksu (Korean Cold Spicy Noodle Salad)

Why This Recipe

Bibim guksu is Korea's go-to summer noodle dish — cold, spicy, tangy buckwheat noodles tossed with crunchy vegetables in a gochujang-based dressing. Perfect for when it's too warm to stand over a hot stove, and a great introduction to gochujang if you haven't used it before.

Ingredients

  • 180g dried buckwheat noodles (or soba noodles)
  • 2 lettuce leaves (butter or oakleaf), thinly sliced
  • 1/4 red cabbage (about 140g), thinly sliced
  • 1/2 small cucumber, julienned
  • 1/3 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 red radish, julienned (optional)
  • A few snow pea sprouts (optional)
  • 2 Tbsp kimchi, cut into thumbnail-sized pieces
  • 2 Tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 Tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 1 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds

Instructions

Prep

  • Thinly slice the lettuce leaves and red cabbage. Set aside.
  • Julienne the cucumber, carrot, and red radish (if using). A mandoline or vegetable peeler works well for uniform strips.
  • Cut the kimchi into small, thumbnail-sized pieces. Pat gently with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.
  • In a small bowl, whisk together gochujang, rice vinegar, soy sauce, honey, brown sugar, sesame oil, and toasted sesame seeds until smooth. Set aside.

Cook

  • Bring a medium pot of water to a rolling boil. Add buckwheat noodles and cook for 3 minutes (or follow package instructions) until just tender.
  • Drain the noodles immediately in a colander.
  • Run cold tap water over the noodles for 1-2 minutes, tossing gently, until completely cooled. This stops the cooking and gives the noodles a firm, springy texture. Drain thoroughly.

Assemble

  • Place the cooled noodles in a large serving bowl.
  • Arrange the sliced lettuce, red cabbage, julienned cucumber, carrot, and radish on top of the noodles.
  • Scatter the kimchi pieces and snow pea sprouts (if using) over the vegetables.

Serve

  • Pour the gochujang sauce evenly over the noodles and vegetables.
  • Using chopsticks, mix everything together thoroughly until the noodles and vegetables are well-coated in the sauce.
  • Serve immediately while the vegetables are still crisp and the noodles are cold.

Pro Tips

  • The sauce is the soul of this dish — taste it before pouring and adjust to your heat preference. For milder spice, reduce gochujang to 1 Tbsp and add an extra Tbsp of honey. For more kick, add 1/2 tsp of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes).
  • For a protein boost, add a halved hard-boiled egg or a handful of canned tuna. The sweet chili-flavored tuna is a classic Korean fridge staple that pairs brilliantly with this dish.
