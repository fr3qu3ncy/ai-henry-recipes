Lomo Saltado (Peruvian Beef Stir Fry)

Why This Recipe

Peruvian-Chinese fusion (Chifa cuisine) introduces a bold new flavor profile — soy-worcestershire-aji amarillo sauce with seared beef, tomatoes, and crispy fries. A completely new cuisine for Martin, with stir-fry technique he knows but in a dramatically different context. Fast (25 min total) and deeply flavorful.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb sirloin steak, sliced against the grain into 1/4-inch strips
  • 3-4 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 small red onion, sliced into thick wedges
  • 2 plum tomatoes, seeds removed, cut into wedges
  • 4 scallions, trimmed and sliced into 2-inch pieces, plus more for garnish
  • 3 garlic cloves, peeled and minced
  • 1/2-inch piece of ginger, peeled and grated (optional but recommended)
  • 1 heaping tablespoon aji amarillo paste (or substitute: 1/4 finely grated serrano + 1/4 finely grated yellow bell pepper)
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh cilantro, plus more for garnish
  • Steamed white rice, for serving
  • French fries (homemade or store-bought frozen), for serving
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • For the marinade:
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar (or red wine vinegar)
  • For the stir fry sauce:
  • 4 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch (optional, for thicker sauce)

Instructions

Marinate the Beef

  • Place the sliced steak in a bowl. Add 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 1/8 teaspoon ground cumin, 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano, and 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar.
  • Toss to coat evenly. Season with a pinch of salt and black pepper.
  • Let the beef marinate for 5-7 minutes while you prep everything else. The actual stir-frying happens fast — have all ingredients measured and ready before turning on the heat.

Prep the Sauce

  • In a small bowl or jar, whisk together 4 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce, 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar, and 1 teaspoon cornstarch (if using).
  • Stir until the cornstarch is fully dissolved with no lumps. Set aside.

Sear the Beef

  • Heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high to high heat. The pan should be very hot — this is key for proper caramelization.
  • Add the beef in a single layer (do not overcrowd — cook in batches if needed). Sear for 2-3 minutes per side until browned and just cooked through.
  • Transfer the seared beef to a plate and set aside. Repeat with remaining beef, adding more oil as needed.

Cook the Vegetables

  • Heat 1 tablespoon oil in the same pan over high heat. Add the red onion wedges and cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened but still crisp. Remove to a plate.
  • Add another tablespoon of oil. Sauté the scallion pieces for 20-30 seconds until fragrant.
  • Add the tomato wedges, minced garlic, grated ginger, and aji amarillo paste. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the tomatoes have released some juices but are still holding their shape.

Combine and Sauce

  • Pour the prepared sauce into the pan with the vegetables. Stir and let it bubble for about 1 minute — the sauce will thicken slightly if you used cornstarch.
  • Return the seared beef (plus any accumulated juices on the plate) and the red onions to the pan.
  • Sprinkle over the minced cilantro. Toss everything gently to coat in the sauce — about 30 seconds.

Serve

  • Serve immediately over steamed white rice with crispy French fries on the side.
  • Garnish with extra sliced scallions and chopped cilantro.
  • Some prefer to toss the fries directly into the stir-fry sauce — feel free to do this for a messier but delicious plate.

Pro Tips

  • Freeze the steak for 10-15 minutes before slicing — it firms up and makes clean, thin cuts much easier. Always slice against the grain for maximum tenderness.
  • Mise en place is critical — the stir-fry cooks in minutes. Have every ingredient chopped, measured, and within arm's reach before you heat the pan.
  • Aji amarillo paste is available at Latin American grocery stores or online (Amazon). If you can't find it, a mix of yellow bell pepper + serrano pepper works, or skip it — the dish will still be delicious.
