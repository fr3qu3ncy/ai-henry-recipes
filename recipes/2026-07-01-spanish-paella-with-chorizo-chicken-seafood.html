Spanish Paella with Chorizo, Chicken & Seafood

Why This Recipe

A proper one-pan Spanish classic that teaches the socarrat technique - that golden crispy rice crust at the bottom is pure magic. Perfect summer dish with seasonal seafood and vibrant saffron colour.

Ingredients

  • 1-2 tbsp olive oil
  • 7 oz / 200g chorizo, sliced 1/2 cm thick
  • 200g squid (calamari), cut into 7mm rings
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 1/2 cups paella rice (Bomba, Valencia, or Calasparra)
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, peeled and diced (or 3/4 cup canned crushed tomato)
  • 300g chicken thigh fillets, cut into 1.5 inch pieces
  • 3 1/2 cups chicken stock (not low sodium)
  • 1 tsp saffron threads (or 1/4 tsp ground saffron)
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 12-16 medium/large prawns, shell on
  • 12 large mussels, cleaned
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 lemons, cut into wedges

Instructions

Sear Proteins

  • Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large 30cm paella pan or wide skillet over high heat.
  • Add chorizo slices and cook until browned on each side, about 3 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  • Add squid rings and cook for 45 seconds on each side, then remove and set aside.

Build the Base

  • Add remaining oil if needed. Add garlic and onion, cook for 2 minutes until fragrant and softened.
  • Add diced red bell pepper, cook for 1 minute.
  • Add chicken pieces. Cook for 2 minutes until lightly browned on the outside but still raw inside.
  • Add paella rice and stir until grains are coated in oil, about 1 minute.

Simmer the Rice

  • Add most of the chorizo (reserve 1/4 for garnish), chicken stock, diced tomatoes, and saffron threads. Stir gently to combine.
  • Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat so it simmers gently - not vigorously, or the rice will burn. Simmer for 10 minutes without stirring. Some rice should poke through the surface with plenty of liquid still visible.

Add Seafood

  • Scatter frozen peas over the rice. Gently push prawns and mussels into the rice - partial submersion is fine.
  • Cook for 8 minutes, turning prawns halfway if needed, until prawns are opaque, mussels are open, and most liquid has been absorbed (a bit should remain). Discard any mussels that do not open.
  • Remove from heat. Taste the rice - it should be firm but cooked. If too firm, add a splash of hot water and continue cooking briefly.

Rest & Serve

  • Scatter reserved chorizo and squid over the top (residual heat will warm them through).
  • Cover with a lid or foil and rest for 5 minutes. The rice will absorb remaining liquid and become tender but juicy, not stodgy.
  • Scatter with chopped parsley and lemon wedges. Serve straight from the pan - mix up the rice with seafood at the table.

Pro Tips

  • The socarrat (golden crispy rice crust on the bottom) is the best part - increase heat for the last 1-2 minutes of cooking to develop it, then listen for a gentle crackling sound. If you hear silence, its perfect.
  • Never stir paella after adding the broth - unlike risotto, stirring activates starch and makes it gluey. The rice should be separate and slightly al dente.
  • Cannot find Bomba rice? Arborio works in a pinch but add an extra 1/2 cup stock and expect a slightly creamier texture.
