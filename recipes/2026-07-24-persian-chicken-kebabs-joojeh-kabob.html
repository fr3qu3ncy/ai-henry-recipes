Persian Chicken Kebabs (Joojeh Kabob)

Why This Recipe

Saffron-scented Persian chicken kebabs are a summer grilling staple — the mayo-based marinade (a true Persian secret) keeps the chicken impossibly tender and juicy. Perfect for BBQ season with that signature golden hue from saffron.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ lbs boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 ½ tbsp lime juice
  • 1 ½ tbsp lemon juice
  • 3 tbsp mayonnaise
  • ½ cup yellow onion, grated or finely minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, grated or finely minced
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • ½ tsp saffron threads (optional but recommended)
  • ¼–½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 ½ tsp kosher salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • For serving: red bell peppers, red onion, Persian cucumbers, fresh herbs, grilled flatbread

Instructions

Bloom the Saffron

  • Crush the saffron threads between your palms into a fine powder in a small bowl.
  • Add 2 tbsp warm water and let sit for 5 minutes until the water turns bright golden-yellow. This 'blooms' the saffron and releases its full flavor.

Make the Marinade

  • In a large bowl, whisk together the bloomed saffron water, olive oil, lime juice, lemon juice, mayonnaise, grated onion, grated garlic, turmeric, red pepper flakes, salt, and black pepper.
  • The mayonnaise is the secret weapon — it tenderizes the chicken far better than yogurt and adds a rich, creamy depth that yogurt can't match.

Marinate the Chicken

  • Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and toss until every piece is thoroughly coated.
  • Transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, ideally overnight (up to 24 hours). The longer the marinate, the more flavorful and tender the chicken.
  • Stir once or twice during marinating to ensure even flavor distribution.

Prep for Grilling

  • If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning. Metal skewers need no soaking and are more durable.
  • Remove chicken from the refrigerator 15 minutes before grilling to take the chill off.
  • Thread chicken pieces onto skewers, alternating with chunks of red bell pepper and red onion if desired. Don't pack too tightly — leave a small gap between pieces for even cooking.

Grill

  • Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (about 375–400°F / 190–205°C). Clean and oil the grates to prevent sticking.
  • Grill the skewers for 8–12 minutes, turning every 2–3 minutes, until the chicken is golden brown with slight char marks and reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).
  • If using an indoor grill pan, heat over medium-high and cook similarly, about 10–14 minutes total.
  • For oven method: Broil on a rack set 4–5 inches from the heating element for 6–8 minutes per side, watching closely to avoid burning.

Serve

  • Serve immediately with Shirazi salad (diced cucumber, tomato, onion, lemon juice, dried mint), fresh herbs, and warm flatbread or saffron rice.
  • Squeeze fresh lemon over the kebabs just before serving for an extra brightness.

Pro Tips

  • The mayonnaise marinade is non-negotiable for authentic joojeh — it creates a protective barrier that keeps the chicken incredibly moist on the grill while adding a subtle richness yogurt can't replicate.
  • Double the marinade and freeze half in an airtight container for up to 3 months. Next time you crave Persian kebabs, just thaw, add fresh chicken, and you're 90% of the way there.
  • Saffron is expensive but worth it — buy whole threads from a reputable Middle Eastern spice shop and grind them fresh each time. Pre-ground saffron loses potency quickly.
