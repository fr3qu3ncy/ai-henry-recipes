Korean Pork Belly Tacos with Corn Cheese

Why This Recipe

Korean-Mexican fusion at its best — crispy pork belly in soft tortillas topped with creamy Korean corn cheese and tangy kimchi. A fun twist on taco night that brings Korean BBQ flavors to your skillet.

Ingredients

  • 12 tortillas (flour or corn)
  • 1 lb pork belly, thinly sliced (shabu-shabu style)
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 4 tsp honey
  • 4 tsp soy sauce
  • 4 tsp granulated sugar, divided (2 + 2)
  • 2 cans (15.25 oz each) whole kernel corn, rinsed and drained
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 cup Napa cabbage kimchi, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup purple cabbage, shredded (optional)
  • 1 jalapeño, thinly sliced (optional)

Instructions

Prep

  • In a mixing bowl, combine gochujang, minced garlic, sesame oil, honey, soy sauce, and 2 tsp sugar. Mix until a smooth, consistent paste forms.
  • Add the thinly sliced pork belly to the sauce and toss until every slice is evenly coated. Let sit for 10-15 minutes while you prep the rest.

Cook the Pork

  • Heat a non-stick skillet or grill plate over medium-high heat.
  • Once hot, add the marinated pork belly and spread it out in an even layer.
  • Cook for 5-7 minutes, turning frequently, until the pork is cooked through and the edges are slightly browned and crispy. Transfer to a plate and set aside.

Make the Corn Cheese

  • In a skillet or saucepan with a lid, combine the corn, butter, mayonnaise, salt, pepper, and remaining 2 tsp sugar.
  • Cook over medium heat, stirring until the butter has melted and everything is evenly combined.
  • Once the corn is slightly bubbling, sprinkle the shredded mozzarella evenly over the top.
  • Cover with the lid and let the cheese melt for about 5 minutes. Stir gently once melted — you should have a creamy, stretchy corn cheese.

Warm the Tortillas

  • If you have a gas stove, hold each tortilla directly over the flame for 10-15 seconds per side until the edges are lightly charred and the tortilla is warm and pliable. Watch closely — they can burn quickly!
  • If using a regular stovetop, warm the tortillas in a dry skillet for 30 seconds per side, or in a countertop oven until warmed through.

Assemble & Serve

  • Lay a warm tortilla flat. Top with a few slices of crispy pork belly.
  • Spoon a generous scoop of corn cheese over the pork.
  • Add chopped kimchi, shredded purple cabbage, and sliced jalapeño.
  • Fold the tortilla and serve immediately. Repeat with remaining ingredients.

Pro Tips

  • Buy shabu-shabu style pork belly from a Korean or Asian market — the paper-thin slices cook in minutes and get beautifully crispy at the edges without needing to be pre-cut.
  • The corn cheese (corn cheddar) is a Korean comfort food staple. For extra richness, add a splash of cream or swap half the mozzarella for cheddar.
